\begin{abstract}
Empirical claims about learned systems inherit the health of the
instruments that measure them. We report six pre-publication incidents from one
measurement-heavy research program
on fast-weight sequence models in which a model, in one case a
synthetic oracle, appeared to
fail and the failure traced instead to the instrument: an admission
tolerance calibrated on the wrong key population; a probe reading a
layer that causal state-zeroing later proved inert; an identity-gauge
assumption measured on one architecture and carried to another; an
uncentered covariance estimator degenerate on the task's
near-orthogonal targets; a target whose ambient identity block taxed
every rank budget; and a cross-check reference invoked on the
transposed side of a state convention. The same discipline adjudicated every incident,
assembled across the catalogue and in full force before the final,
decisive one:
instrument-health adjudication before any model verdict,
pre-registered crosschecks graded where the primary lens's assumptions
fail, falsifier controls run to completion, and raw-artifact
tiebreaks. The discipline is itself falsifiable; its decisive
crosscheck survived a pre-registered shuffled-target control that
would have voided the adjudication. It does not launder results: the
corrected lens left the endpoint verdict standing and
two genuine model failures intact. We argue the discipline transfers
to any empirical evaluation gated by numerical checks, learned probes,
or basis-dependent readouts.
\end{abstract}


\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\looseness=-1 In one research program's five-day window on fast-weight sequence
models, six apparent model failures traced, under the same
adjudication procedure, to broken instruments rather than models
(Table~\ref{tab:catalogue}); in two further readings it confirmed
the model, not the lens, had failed. An incident
here is a pre-registered endpoint or evaluation round read as a model
verdict; defects caught outside such a reading (the
auxiliary-classifier null of \S\ref{sec:wronglayer}) are noted, not
counted. An evaluation pipeline that never suspects its own
instruments will publish lens artifacts as model results.

Our contributions: (1)~the catalogue (Table~\ref{tab:catalogue}), each
incident with its mechanism
and a reusable diagnostic signature; (2)~a five-rule
adjudication discipline (\S\ref{sec:discipline}) that caught all six
before publication; (3)~the discipline's boundary: it did not flip the
pre-registered endpoint verdict, and two genuine model failures
survived every instrument fix (\S\ref{sec:notflipped}).


\section{The Adjudication Discipline}
\label{sec:discipline}

None of the five rules is novel in isolation; applied together,
\emph{before} any model verdict is recorded, they form a lens-failure
detector with a measured record. Two clauses were codified
mid-catalogue by the incidents that exposed them (Rule 3's
positive-control clause, \S\ref{sec:wronglayer}; Rule 2's graded
crosscheck, Appendix~\ref{app:threelenses}); all five rules were in
force before the decisive adjudication of \S\ref{sec:gauge}.

\textbf{Rule 1: instrument-health adjudication precedes model
verdicts.} Before a sweep's pre-registered endpoint may be read, a
health checklist walks the raw per-cell artifacts: gate violations,
convergence, a contradiction scan between independent readouts (the
rule that fired in \S\ref{sec:gauge}).

\textbf{Rule 2: pre-register a crosscheck that removes the primary
lens's strongest assumption}, graded wherever that assumption provably
fails, the switching rule fixed in advance.

\textbf{Rule 3: falsifier teeth, run to completion.} Every negative
control is executed, not merely written; guards are mutation-tested;
every probe rung carries a planted-signal positive control; the
adjudication itself carries a pre-registered falsifier whose firing
voids it.

\textbf{Rule 4: contradictions are resolved against raw artifacts.}
When two reads disagree, neither recency nor authority decides: both
are recomputed from the committed raw files and the tiebreak is
recorded before anything downstream runs.

\textbf{Rule 5: argmax closure.} Wherever a claim requires exact
continuous recovery, the grading path must not be satisfiable by
argmax decoding, under which a rank-1 matrix can recover on the order
of $d$ associations \citep{nichani2025factual}; the converse gap was
pre-registered before it fired (\S\ref{sec:wronglayer}).

\section{Case I: The Tolerance That Manufactured a Cliff}
\label{sec:tolerance}

A fast-weight associative memory's recall capacity is measured by
exact continuous recovery (cosine $\geq0.9$, never argmax; Rule 5)
across a load sweep $K/\dstate$; an eval-time admission gate decides
which trained cells enter a capacity fit; one leg checks that a
Newton--Schulz orthogonalization in the scoring path converges to
residual $<0.01$ within $\niter=20$ iterations. A wide-grid sweep at
$\dstate=96$ trained cleanly at every cell (training guard $1.0$
throughout), yet only one of 12 new cells was admissible
($K{=}78$, $84$, $90$: all three seeds
failing): the shape of a capacity cliff, contradicting the same
architecture's established super-linear growth at lower state
sizes~\citep{companion2026capacity} and the same grid's own post-fix
re-fit below.

The first mechanistic diagnosis blamed the trained anchor table's
convergence at high load; a follow-up refuted it with margins
(residuals at most $1.4\times10^{-6}$, over $7{,}000\times$ below
tolerance,
identical across failing and passing cells; failures confined to a
pool the anchor table bypasses) and the claim was formally
retracted.

\looseness=-1 A purpose-built repro instrument recovered the true failing
tensor: the pool's raw keys, never saved by the original checkpoint
writer. Its decision rule, a pre-registered strict precedence order,
was walked mechanically (Table~\ref{tab:walk}): every
instrument-bug branch failed to fire, and an iteration sweep resolved
all 4{,}608 flagged episodes by $\niter\le28$.
A wall keeps failing as the budget grows; a miscalibrated tolerance
resolves at a bump, and did. Root cause: a
tolerance calibrated on anchor-stabilized \emph{blended} keys,
silently applied to the structurally different \emph{raw} keys this
one pool queries. Only the admission flag was
recalibrated, only for the 11 cells verified to share the identical
signature; the re-fit found no cliff
through $K/\dstate=0.9375$ (per-load mean recovery $0.92$ to $1.00$).

\section{Case II: The Instrument at the Wrong Layer}
\label{sec:wronglayer}

\looseness=-1 A two-block fast-weight model is trained on an associative binding
task ($K=32$ bindings per episode) against matched baselines, with two
registered readouts: a discrete task metric (episode-restricted recall
through the model's own LM head) and a continuous
probe metric $\rf$ (the fraction of targets a linear probe on a
tapped state recovers at cosine $\geq0.9$). In the third of three
consecutive rounds recorded as failures at $\rf=0.0$, the discrete
metric dissociated: recall accuracy $0.9990$ against chance $0.03125$
(baselines $0.0447$ and $0.0295$), the probe still exactly $0.0$ in
every arm; the dissociation, not the zero, is the finding (Rule 5).

\looseness=-1 An offline re-fit battery removed the probe-training explanations:
closed-form ridge regression, pinned SGD, and MLP probes all
reproduced $\rf=0.0$ exactly (over-regularization ruled out by the
rig's post-nonlinearity recovery below); the trained probe's
output was the analytically known
episode-membership direction (mean cosine $0.176$ against the
$1/\sqrt{32}\approx0.177$ ceiling; membership cosine $0.896$); the
ridge fit cleared its shuffled-tap control~\citep{hewitt2019control}
by at most $+0.059$
cosine.
Meanwhile the full forward pass decodes the answer at $0.9957$
accuracy ($31.9\times$ chance) for the contender, both baselines at or
near chance: the answer exists in the network.

\looseness=-1 A state-zeroing experiment~\citep{elazar2021amnesic} located the
storage
(Figure~\ref{fig:taploc}): zeroing the
first block's state $S_0$ collapses recall to chance ($0.0286$);
zeroing $S_1$, the layer every deployed instrument tapped, leaves it
bit-unchanged at $0.9990$: the probed layer is causally inert.
Placement alone is not the whole defect: a ridge
probe on the load-bearing $S_0$ \emph{also} reads $\rf=0.0$
(shuffled-control gaps $+0.002$ to $+0.063$),
and only the post-block-1, pre-LM-head hidden exposes the recall
($\rf=0.674$, gap $+0.800$), only in the arm that performs
the task (ablation: $0.0$; Table~\ref{tab:taps}).
The bindings are stored in a format no tested linear read exposes;
the model's own downstream nonlinearity is the only decoder found.

The same diagnosis exposed a second defect in passing: an auxiliary
classifier whose labels were uniform given the decoded quantity, so a
perfect tap scores chance; its expected-null had been read as
confirmation for a round, hence Rule 3's positive-control clause.

\section{Case III: The Gauge That Did Not Transfer}
\label{sec:gauge}

\looseness=-1 A recurrent composer is trained on the word problems of five finite
groups and scored for compositional depth generalization: trained at
depth $\le8$, tested at depth 64. Two pre-registered lenses score
recovery of the group representation from the state: the
\emph{primary} de-gauges with a scale-only correction, valid when the
learned basis matches the target up to scale
($\hat{Q}\approx I$, measured on every checkpoint of the
\emph{previous} architecture); the \emph{crosscheck} fits a full
orthogonal intertwiner by Procrustes, fit and scored on disjoint
splits, assuming nothing about the gauge.

\looseness=-1 \textbf{The signature.} The 62-cell sweep completed cleanly; run as
built on the primary lens, the endpoint machinery returned
\textsc{falsify}: no measurable separation from the baseline at the
decisive groups.
The mandatory instrument-health pass (Rule 1) surfaced a
contradiction (Figure~\ref{fig:lens}): wherever training had
\emph{not} converged, both lenses read near zero; wherever it
converged, they contradicted each other at full magnitude (one
converged cell: loss $0.0001$, primary $0.050$,
crosscheck $1.000$). All 13 cells with a lens disagreement of at
least $0.5$ are converged cells (threshold-robust: every cell with a
disagreement above $0.3$ is converged, 16 of 16); on all 25 Arm-2
baseline cells (5 groups $\times$ 5 seeds, 50 checkpoint values) the
lenses agree exactly. The harvest stopped for adjudication with no
model verdict recorded.

\looseness=-1 \textbf{The tiebreak.} A leaky crosscheck reading high on junk would
leave the falsification standing. The adjudication, against raw
artifacts and code
(Rule 4), rested on four grounds:
(1)~the crosscheck is leakage-guarded by construction (fit and eval
on disjoint splits); (2)~it
discriminates, reading near zero on every non-converged
cell; (3)~the primary's scale-only correction was already flagged as
basis-brittle across seeds (per-seed mean cosine $0.03$ to $0.69$
where the crosscheck read $0.86$ to $0.95$) and its
$\hat{Q}\approx I$ evidence came from a different architecture (a
perfect model in a rotated basis reads zero under an identity gauge);
(4)~the synthetic acceptance test already grades the crosscheck when
the injected gauge is non-identity (Rule 2).
Verdict: the primary lens is broken on converged cells; the mechanical
falsification is void \emph{as a model verdict}.

\looseness=-1 \textbf{Teeth.} The tiebreak carried its own pre-registered falsifier
(Rule 3): shuffled-target re-scoring must read near zero, or the
tiebreak is wrong and the contradiction escalates to an instrument
rebuild. On three pre-registered converged checkpoints spanning
three groups (among them the converged exemplar above), the shuffled
crosscheck read $0.00$, $0.00$, and
$0.05$, far below the $0.5$ void threshold, while the unshuffled
reads reproduced the committed values bit-identically
(Table~\ref{tab:teeth}).

\textbf{The re-metric.} The full grid was re-read under the crosscheck
lens through the unmodified verdict machinery, which first reproduced
the committed primary verdict and all 62 recomputed ceilings to within
$10^{-9}$.
The corrected endpoint keeps the same \textsc{falsify} verdict
\emph{and the same machine-emitted reason}
(\S\ref{sec:notflipped}, Table~\ref{tab:remetric}); what the fix
changes is the readable geometry: the primary was not a blanket
null (S3 cleared its bar, $0.54$ against $0.459$) but masked which
groups fail, while under the crosscheck the two decisive
failures, an S5 near-miss and an A6 non-convergence, stand out
lens-independently. Fixing the instrument changed what the verdict
means, not the verdict.

\section{Three More Lenses, Briefly}
\label{sec:threelenses}

\looseness=-1 Three further incidents (rows 4--6 of Table~\ref{tab:catalogue};
numbers in Appendix~\ref{app:threelenses}): an
\textbf{uncentered covariance}, degenerate on the task's own
near-orthogonal targets, that failed a synthetic \emph{perfect} model
(the rescue de-confounds into centering plus a full-intertwiner
degauge);
a \textbf{target with an ambient identity block}, under which a
causal rank experiment returned near-chance
recovery at every rank (zero in four of five groups; $0.17$ and
$0.08$ in the fifth) while the capped arms sat at the exact rank-$k$
ceiling $\sqrt{k/\dstate}$: undelivered by the
instrument, not failed by the model;
and a \textbf{reference kernel invoked on the transposed side} of a
state convention, ``contradicting'' a correct recurrence at the
$\sqrt{2}$ signature of a transposed outer product, exonerated by a
closed-form hand computation.

\section{The Boundary: What the Discipline Did Not Flip}
\label{sec:notflipped}

\looseness=-1 A lens-repair procedure that only rescued the preferred hypothesis
would be a laundering machine; under the corrected
lens, Case III's endpoint remained
\textsc{falsify}, held there by two lens-independent model results:
one decisive configuration never reaches training-support convergence
(recovery $0.00$ at depth 64, all five seeds, both
lenses), and the other decisive group sits below its pre-registered
bar ($0.483$ against $0.735$) lens-independently: one seed
generalizes at $0.00$ under both lenses despite low training loss,
and even its siblings reach only $80\%$ and $65\%$ of their own
ceilings, under the $90\%$ criterion, so the group misses its bar
with or without the outlier, against a baseline pinned at $0.0$.

Case I shows the same refusal: a diagnosed pool-margin confound
($+0.0330$, $12\times$ the $0.0028$ noise floor) was withheld because
the diagnostic's own cells never cleared admission, and an archived
exact-ceiling reading ($1.0000$, three seeds) that did not replicate
at a fresh seed ($0.9725$) was re-scoped to the seeds measured; the
machinery that unlocked eleven quarantined cells declined, twice, to
promote findings it could not support.

\section{Related Work and Limitations}
\label{sec:related}

\looseness=-1 \textbf{Related work.} \citet{silberzahn2018many} and
\citet{damour2020underspecification} document divergent conclusions
from underspecified pipelines; here the models are fixed and
the \emph{measurement pipeline} is the adjudicated object.
\citet{henderson2018deep} and
\citet{bouthillier2021accounting} quantify seed and tuning variance;
none of the six defects is sampling variance, so variance
accounting catches none. REFORMS
\citep{kapoor2023reforms}, \citet{sculley2018winners}, and
\citet{lipton2018troubling} prescribe what a trustworthy paper
discloses; we supply the in-flight machinery such checklists
presume exists. The probing literature supplies the
instrument-hygiene checks the wrong-layer case rediscovers at
evaluation scale: control tasks and
selectivity~\citep{hewitt2019control}, the probe-versus-behavior
dissociation~\citep{belinkov2022probing}, amnesic
probing~\citep{elazar2021amnesic}, and randomization-based sanity
checks~\citep{adebayo2018sanity}. \citet{schaeffer2023emergent}
argue the inverse of our thesis (an apparent model \emph{property},
emergence, as metric artifact) from the same
instrument-first stance; each diagnoses one instrument
class, where this catalogue is a serial, pre-registered,
cross-instrument adjudication record with falsifier teeth.
\citet{nichani2025factual} prove Rule 5's argmax/exact-recovery gap;
we report it firing live (\S\ref{sec:wronglayer}); the fast-weight
testbeds follow \citet{schlag2021linear}.

\looseness=-1 \textbf{Limitations.} All six incidents come from one program, one
team, one architecture family: they support no prevalence claim, and
we cannot report the rate a lighter process would miss.
The catalogue is survivor-biased: a defect missed by both crosscheck
and falsifier battery appears in no record we could write. It is
biased by trigger too: the discipline fires on apparent model
failure, so defects flattering the model are under-represented; five
of six fixes moved results in the program-favorable direction. In the
one incident costed, the two diagnostic instruments took $0.50$ and
$1.10$ GPU-hours (archived wall-clock) against the $6.33$ GPU-hours
of training the uncorrected readings would have discredited. We
report no false-alarm rate: the records do not tally escalations
against clean instrument-health passes, so the precision of
``suspect the instrument first'' is unmeasured.

\section{The Three Brief Lenses, in Full}
\label{app:threelenses}

\textbf{The covariance that could not see.} An acceptance test for a
representation-recovery pipeline injected a synthetic \emph{perfect}
model and failed it. The subspace step estimated an uncentered
covariance, degenerate on the task's own targets: for near-orthogonal
states $Z\approx c(\rho\oplus I)$, $ZZ^{\top}\approx c^{2}I$ is
isotropic and carries no subspace information. On the production
harness, the headline rescue of the oracle injection (mean cosine
$0.084$ to $0.9996$) folds in two registered lens changes: centering
\emph{and} a switch from the scale-only primary to the
full-intertwiner crosscheck. Isolating centering under a matched
full-intertwiner metric moves mean cosine $0.705$ to $0.9996$; under
the scale-only lens the centered pipeline reads $0.046$, no better
than the uncentered $0.084$, because the injection's true gauge is
non-identity, the pre-echo of \S\ref{sec:gauge}. Centering is a
genuine fix, but the full rescue is centering plus a full-intertwiner
degauge, not centering alone.
The fix (centering) cancels the constant identity block because the
group mean of a nontrivial representation vanishes; the acceptance
injection was extended so the previously skipped subspace step now
sits inside the test.

\textbf{The target that taxed every budget.} A causal test of a
rank-recruitment law capped model rank at $k$ around the predicted
minimum $\dmin$ and expected recovery to return at $k=\dmin$. Every
capped arm returned near-chance recovery on both lenses: zero in four
of the five groups, and $0.17$/$0.08$ in the fifth (S3), far below
that group's ceiling. The target
had been constructed as $\rho\oplus I$: the ambient identity block
inflates the as-built target rank by two, so every rank budget buys
the constant block first. The diagnosis is quantitative: a rank-$k$
model's best achievable cosine against this target is exactly
$\sqrt{k/\dstate}$, and 37 of 39 force-rank cells sat within $0.07$ of
that ceiling (mean absolute deviation $0.028$); the arms had trained
to their theoretical optimum against the wrong target.
The causal test was undelivered by the instrument, not failed by the
model; a corrected-target, zero-padded re-run subsequently delivered
the test (companion work, anonymized)~\citep{companion2026capacity}.

\textbf{The reference on the wrong side.} A bespoke recurrence was
cross-checked against a reference kernel and disagreed at relative
Frobenius residual $1.40$; the recurrence was the initial suspect. At
depth one the update is closed-form ($S_1=\beta vk^{\top}$), so a hand
computation with scalar loops adjudicates conventions with no
numerics: the composer matched the closed form at $4.5\times10^{-8}$
and its \emph{transpose} at $1.405$, and for near-orthogonal unit $k$
the transpose signature is exactly
$\|vk^{\top}-kv^{\top}\|_F/\|vk^{\top}\|_F\approx\sqrt{2}$. The
reference library's documented state convention is the transpose of
the pinned equation; the cross-check's own reference invocation was
the defect. With the returned state transposed before comparison, the
cross-check passes all three pinned configurations (residuals
$2.8\times10^{-3}$ to $4.5\times10^{-3}$), and a permanent negative
test confirms a transposed-update mutant is killed at $1.405$.
