\section{Case II: The Instrument at the Wrong Layer}
\label{sec:wronglayer}

\looseness=-1 A two-block fast-weight model is trained on an associative binding
task ($K=32$ bindings per episode) against matched baselines, with two
registered readouts: a discrete task metric (episode-restricted recall
through the model's own LM head) and a continuous
probe metric $\rf$ (the fraction of targets a linear probe on a
tapped state recovers at cosine $\geq0.9$). In the third of three
consecutive rounds recorded as failures at $\rf=0.0$, the discrete
metric dissociated: recall accuracy $0.9990$ against chance $0.03125$
(baselines $0.0447$ and $0.0295$), the probe still exactly $0.0$ in
every arm; the dissociation, not the zero, is the finding (Rule 5).
% <!-- evidence: W1 -->

\looseness=-1 An offline re-fit battery removed the probe-training explanations:
closed-form ridge regression, pinned SGD, and MLP probes all
reproduced $\rf=0.0$ exactly (over-regularization ruled out by the
rig's post-nonlinearity recovery below); the trained probe's
output was the analytically known
episode-membership direction (mean cosine $0.176$ against the
$1/\sqrt{32}\approx0.177$ ceiling; membership cosine $0.896$); the
ridge fit cleared its shuffled-tap control~\citep{hewitt2019control}
by at most $+0.059$
cosine.
% <!-- evidence: W2 -->
Meanwhile the full forward pass decodes the answer at $0.9957$
accuracy ($31.9\times$ chance) for the contender, both baselines at or
near chance: the answer exists in the network.
% <!-- evidence: W3 -->

\looseness=-1 A state-zeroing experiment~\citep{elazar2021amnesic} located the
storage
(Figure~\ref{fig:taploc}): zeroing the
first block's state $S_0$ collapses recall to chance ($0.0286$);
zeroing $S_1$, the layer every deployed instrument tapped, leaves it
bit-unchanged at $0.9990$. That layer plays no causal role in the
recall.
Placement alone is not the whole defect: a ridge
probe on the load-bearing $S_0$ \emph{also} reads $\rf=0.0$
(shuffled-control gaps $+0.002$ to $+0.063$),
and only the post-block-1, pre-LM-head hidden exposes the recall
($\rf=0.674$, gap $+0.800$), only in the arm that performs
the task (ablation: $0.0$; Table~\ref{tab:taps}).
% <!-- evidence: W4 -->
The bindings are stored in a format no tested linear read exposes;
the model's own downstream nonlinearity is the only decoder found.

The same diagnosis exposed a second defect in passing: an auxiliary
classifier whose labels were uniform given the decoded quantity, so a
perfect tap scores chance; its expected-null had been read as
confirmation for a round, hence Rule 3's positive-control clause.
% <!-- evidence: W5 -->
