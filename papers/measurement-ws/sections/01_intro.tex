\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\looseness=-1 In one research program's five-day window on fast-weight sequence
models, six apparent model failures traced, under the same
adjudication procedure, to broken instruments rather than models
(Table~\ref{tab:catalogue}); in two further readings it confirmed
the model, not the lens, had failed. An incident
here is a pre-registered endpoint or evaluation round read as a model
verdict; defects caught outside such a reading (the
auxiliary-classifier null of \S\ref{sec:wronglayer}) are noted, not
counted. An evaluation pipeline that never suspects its own
instruments will publish lens artifacts as model results.

Our contributions: (1)~the catalogue (Table~\ref{tab:catalogue}), each
incident with its mechanism
and a reusable diagnostic signature; (2)~a five-rule
adjudication discipline (\S\ref{sec:discipline}) that caught all six
before publication; (3)~the discipline's boundary: it did not flip the
pre-registered endpoint verdict, and two genuine model failures
survived every instrument fix (\S\ref{sec:notflipped}).
