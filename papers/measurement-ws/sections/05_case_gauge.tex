\section{Case III: The Gauge That Did Not Transfer}
\label{sec:gauge}

\looseness=-1 A recurrent composer is trained on the word problems of five finite
groups and scored for compositional depth generalization: trained at
depth $\le8$, tested at depth 64. Two pre-registered lenses score
recovery of the group representation from the state: the
\emph{primary} de-gauges with a scale-only correction, valid when the
learned basis matches the target up to scale
($\hat{Q}\approx I$, measured on every checkpoint of the
\emph{previous} architecture); the \emph{crosscheck} fits a full
orthogonal intertwiner by Procrustes, fit and scored on disjoint
splits, assuming nothing about the gauge.

\looseness=-1 \textbf{The signature.} The 62-cell sweep completed cleanly; run as
built on the primary lens, the endpoint machinery returned
\textsc{falsify}: no measurable separation from the baseline at the
decisive groups.
% <!-- evidence: X1 -->
The mandatory instrument-health pass (Rule 1) surfaced a
contradiction (Figure~\ref{fig:lens}): wherever training had
\emph{not} converged, both lenses read near zero; wherever it
converged, they contradicted each other at full magnitude (one
converged cell: loss $0.0001$, primary $0.050$,
crosscheck $1.000$). All 13 cells with a lens disagreement of at
least $0.5$ are converged cells (threshold-robust: every cell with a
disagreement above $0.3$ is converged, 16 of 16); on all 25 Arm-2
baseline cells (5 groups $\times$ 5 seeds, 50 checkpoint values) the
lenses agree exactly. The harvest stopped for adjudication with no
model verdict recorded.
% <!-- evidence: X2 -->

\looseness=-1 \textbf{The tiebreak.} A leaky crosscheck reading high on junk would
leave the falsification standing. The adjudication, against raw
artifacts and code
(Rule 4), rested on four grounds:
(1)~the crosscheck is leakage-guarded by construction (fit and eval
on disjoint splits); (2)~it
discriminates, reading near zero on every non-converged
cell; (3)~the primary's scale-only correction was already flagged as
basis-brittle across seeds (per-seed mean cosine $0.03$ to $0.69$
where the crosscheck read $0.86$ to $0.95$) and its
$\hat{Q}\approx I$ evidence came from a different architecture (a
perfect model in a rotated basis reads zero under an identity gauge);
% <!-- evidence: X3 -->
(4)~the synthetic acceptance test already grades the crosscheck when
the injected gauge is non-identity (Rule 2).
Verdict: the primary lens is broken on converged cells; the mechanical
falsification is void \emph{as a model verdict}.

\looseness=-1 \textbf{Teeth.} The tiebreak carried its own pre-registered falsifier
(Rule 3): shuffled-target re-scoring must read near zero, or the
tiebreak is wrong and the contradiction escalates to an instrument
rebuild. On three pre-registered converged checkpoints spanning
three groups (among them the converged exemplar above), the shuffled
crosscheck read $0.00$, $0.00$, and
$0.05$, far below the $0.5$ void threshold, while the unshuffled
reads reproduced the committed values bit-identically
(Table~\ref{tab:teeth}).
% <!-- evidence: X4 -->

\textbf{The re-metric.} The full grid was re-read under the crosscheck
lens through the unmodified verdict machinery, which first reproduced
the committed primary verdict and all 62 recomputed ceilings to within
$10^{-9}$.
% <!-- evidence: X5 -->
The corrected endpoint keeps the same \textsc{falsify} verdict
\emph{and the same machine-emitted reason}
(\S\ref{sec:notflipped}, Table~\ref{tab:remetric}); what the fix
changes is the readable geometry: the primary was not a blanket
null (S3 cleared its bar, $0.54$ against $0.459$) but masked which
groups fail, while under the crosscheck the two decisive
failures, an S5 near-miss and an A6 non-convergence, stand out
lens-independently. Fixing the instrument changed what the verdict
means, not the verdict.
