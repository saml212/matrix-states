\section{Case I: The Tolerance That Manufactured a Cliff}
\label{sec:tolerance}

A fast-weight associative memory's recall capacity is measured by
exact continuous recovery (cosine $\geq0.9$, never argmax; Rule 5)
across a load sweep $K/\dstate$. An eval-time admission gate decides
which trained cells enter a capacity fit. One leg of that gate checks
that a Newton--Schulz orthogonalization in the scoring path converges
to residual $<0.01$ within $\niter=20$ iterations. A wide-grid sweep at
$\dstate=96$ trained cleanly at every cell (training guard $1.0$
throughout), yet only one of 12 new cells was admissible
($K{=}78$, $84$, $90$: all three seeds
failing): the shape of a capacity cliff, contradicting the same
architecture's established super-linear growth at lower state
sizes~\citep{companion2026capacity} and the same grid's own post-fix
re-fit below.
% <!-- evidence: I1 -->

The first mechanistic diagnosis blamed the trained anchor table's
convergence at high load; a follow-up refuted it with margins
(residuals at most $1.6\times10^{-6}$, over $6{,}000\times$ below
tolerance,
indistinguishable across failing and passing cells; failures confined
to a
pool the anchor table bypasses) and the claim was formally
retracted.
% <!-- evidence: I2 -->

\looseness=-1 A purpose-built repro instrument recovered the true failing
tensor: the pool's raw keys, never saved by the original checkpoint
writer. Its decision rule, a pre-registered strict precedence order,
was walked mechanically (Table~\ref{tab:walk}): every
instrument-bug branch failed to fire, and an iteration sweep resolved
all 4{,}608 flagged episodes by $\niter\le28$.
% <!-- evidence: I3 -->
A wall keeps failing as the budget grows; a miscalibrated tolerance
resolves at a bump, and did. Root cause: a
tolerance calibrated on anchor-stabilized \emph{blended} keys,
silently applied to the structurally different \emph{raw} keys this
one pool queries. Only the admission flag was
recalibrated, only for the 11 cells verified to share the identical
signature; the re-fit found no cliff
through $K/\dstate=0.9375$ (per-load mean recovery $0.92$ to $1.00$).
% <!-- evidence: I4 -->
