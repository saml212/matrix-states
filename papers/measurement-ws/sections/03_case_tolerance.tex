\section{Case I: The Tolerance That Manufactured a Cliff}
\label{sec:tolerance}

A fast-weight associative memory's recall capacity is measured by
exact continuous recovery (cosine $\geq0.9$, never argmax; Rule 5)
across a load sweep $K/\dstate$; an eval-time admission gate decides
which trained cells may enter a capacity fit, one leg checking that a
Newton--Schulz orthogonalization inside the scoring path converges to
residual $<0.01$ within $\niter=20$ iterations. A wide-grid sweep at
$\dstate=96$ trained cleanly at every cell (the training guard read
exactly $1.0$ throughout), yet of 12 new cells only one was
admissible, with $K{=}78$, $84$, and $90$ failing on all three seeds
each: the shape of a capacity cliff, contradicting the same
architecture's established super-linear growth at lower state
sizes~\citep{companion2026capacity}.
% <!-- evidence: I1 -->

The first mechanistic diagnosis blamed the trained anchor table's
convergence at high load; a follow-up refuted it with margins
(residuals near $1.2\times10^{-6}$, roughly $7{,}800\times$ below
tolerance, identical between failing and passing cells, failures
confined to one pool the anchor table architecturally bypasses) and
the claim was formally retracted.
% <!-- evidence: I2 -->

\looseness=-1 A purpose-built repro instrument then recovered the true failing
tensor: the pool's raw keys, never saved by the original checkpoint
writer. Its decision rule was pre-registered as a strict precedence
order and walked mechanically (Table~\ref{tab:walk}); every
instrument-bug branch failed to fire, and an iteration sweep resolved
every one of the 4{,}608 flagged episodes by $\niter\le28$.
% <!-- evidence: I3 -->
A wall keeps failing as the budget grows; a miscalibrated tolerance
resolves at a fixed bump, and did. Root cause: a
tolerance calibrated against anchor-stabilized \emph{blended} keys,
silently applied to the structurally different \emph{raw} key
population this one pool queries. Only the admission flag was
recalibrated, only for the 11 cells verified to share the identical
signature, at zero additional GPU cost; the re-fit found no cliff
through $K/\dstate=0.9375$ (per-load mean recovery $0.92$ to $1.00$).
% <!-- evidence: I4 -->
