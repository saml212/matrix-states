# 6. Related Work

Zoology [Arora et al. 2023] and Based [Arora et al. 2024] establish that
fixed-state models trade recall against state size and set the
param-matched ladder pattern this program's scale series follows; both
study architectures trained for the probe setting, where this program
probes general-purpose checkpoints post hoc and reports on instrument
validity rather than a capacity frontier. Okpekpe and Orvieto [2025] give
a positive causal account of recall differences between Transformers and
state-space models; this paper makes no positive mechanism claim and
instead bounds one named mechanism, write-geometry stabilization, three
ways. Nichani et al. [2025] provide the associative-memory analysis behind
this program's exact-recovery readout choice: under argmax decoding a
low-rank state can decode far more associations than it structurally
holds, so a rank-sensitive claim needs continuous recovery, which is the
readout scored here. The delta-rule state itself follows Schlag et al.
[2021] and Yang et al. [2024]. On the reporting side, the paper follows
the pre-registration and negative-results practice argued for in the
machine-learning community by the NeurIPS pre-registration workshops
[Bertinetto et al. 2021] and the negative-results venue tradition
[Forde et al. 2020]: outcome partitions fixed before unblinding, and
refusals reported as results.
