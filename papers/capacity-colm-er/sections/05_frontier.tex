\section{The Capacity Frontier Grows Super-Linearly}
\label{sec:frontier}

If the transition of Section~\ref{sec:act1} were a property of the ratio
$K/d_{\mathrm{state}}$, its location would be invariant across scales. The
invariance band $[0.4745, 0.6165]$, centered on the
$d_{\mathrm{state}}=64$ midpoint, was registered before any larger-scale
cell ran. % evidence: C10

\paragraph{The frontier at $d_{\mathrm{state}}=80$.} With loads
$K \in \{20, 43, 48, 53, 58\}$ at three seeds each, escalated to five
seeds at the two mid-transition loads, the refit locates
$x_0(80) = 0.6779$ (95\% CI $[0.6683, 0.6867]$, $w = 0.0479$, 0 of
4{,}000 bootstrap fits degenerate). % evidence: C10
The CI excludes the invariance band entirely, on the high side: in ratio
terms the frontier moved up by $+0.13$ as $d_{\mathrm{state}}$ grew from
64 to 80. % evidence: C10
Ratio invariance is refuted, in the direction of \emph{more} usable load
per dimension at larger scale (Figure~\ref{fig:frontier}).

\paragraph{No transition through $K/d = 0.9375$ at
$d_{\mathrm{state}}=96$.} The registered $d_{\mathrm{state}}=96$ grid
($K = 24/51/57/63/69$, three seeds) is flat near ceiling across
$K/d \in [0.25, 0.71875]$, with per-load means
$1.0/1.0/0.9974/0.9805/0.9839$; its sigmoid fit is degenerate ($98.5\%$
of bootstrap resamples) and is disclosed as such, never quoted as a
location. % evidence: C16
A wider grid ($K = 69/72/78/84/90$, three seeds) initially returned 11 of
its 12 new cells inadmissible at the standing admission gate. An
independent diagnostic instrument traced the failures to a tolerance
miscalibration in the gate's convergence check, not to the cells: every
one of the 4{,}608 flagged evaluation episodes resolves by
$n_{\mathrm{iter}} \le 28$ against the production setting of 20.
% evidence: C12
Only the admission flag was recalibrated (11 cells flipped); no $h4$
measurement changed and no cell re-ran. % evidence: C12
A thirteenth examined cell, a pre-existing $K{=}69$ seed from an earlier
wave with the identical failing signature, was deliberately excluded per
the recalibration's declared 11-cell scope; that cell reads
$h4 = 0.9918$, so including it would add a fourth $K{=}69$ seed and
raise that load's mean from $0.9592$ to $0.9673$, changing no
conclusion. % evidence: C20
On the unlocked grid the per-load means are
$0.9592/0.9216/0.9326/0.9581/1.0000$ across
$K/d \in [0.71875, 0.9375]$: no monotonic collapse anywhere in the
window, a non-monotonic dip-and-recovery instead, and a fit that is
$100\%$ bootstrap-degenerate from genuine per-seed scatter (the noisiest
load, $K{=}72$, spans $0.8426$--$0.9904$ across seeds). % evidence: C11
The supportable summary is a window-limited bound: at
$d_{\mathrm{state}}=96$, no transition through $K/d = 0.9375$, with the
fine structure near ceiling left unresolved
(Section~\ref{sec:limitations}).

\paragraph{Scaling in bytes, not width.} Writing
$K^{*} = x_0 \cdot d_{\mathrm{state}}$ for the located midpoint load in
raw bindings, the two located frontiers give $K^{*} = 34.9$ at
$d_{\mathrm{state}}=64$ and $K^{*} = 54.2$ at $d_{\mathrm{state}}=80$.
% evidence: C15
The two-point growth exponent is
$p = \ln(54.23/34.91)/\ln(80/64) = 1.97$, with CI-propagated range
$[1.86, 2.09]$ from the two fits' bootstrap intervals: % evidence: C15
consistent with $p = 2$, capacity proportional to state \emph{bytes}
($4d^2$ at fp32), and excluding $p = 1$, capacity proportional to state
width. Table~\ref{tab:bytes} states the same arithmetic per byte. Two
registered functional forms for the growth remain live and undiscriminated
at $d_{\mathrm{state}}=96$, where the fit is degenerate: an
absolute-slack account predicting $x_0(96) \in [0.718, 0.739]$ and a
power-law account predicting $[0.768, 0.837]$. % evidence: C13
Both bands sit inside the flat measured window, so neither is excluded;
discriminating them is registered follow-on work, not claimed here.

For a deployment engineer the reading is direct: over the measured range,
spending bytes on a larger fast-weight state buys bindings at an
approximately constant per-byte rate, and the safe load \emph{ratio}
tuned at a small state under-provisions a larger one by a CI-excluding
margin. % evidence: C15
