\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\paragraph{Scope.} All results use a single-head, single-layer DeltaNet,
one synthetic 107-entity recall grammar, sub-million-parameter models, and
$d_{\mathrm{state}} \in \{64, 80, 96, 128\}$
($d_{\mathrm{state}} = 32$ is excluded by a kernel-level failure in the
underlying library, measured independently of this program). No claim
transfers to production-scale gated variants
\citep{siems2025deltaproduct, grazzi2025negative}, to matrix-state models
at language-model scale \citep{mishra2026m2rnn}, or to natural language.
The per-byte conservation reading of Table~\ref{tab:bytes} rests on two
located points plus one in-window bound; it is a measured regularity over
$d_{\mathrm{state}} \in [64, 96]$, not a law.

\paragraph{The frontier's functional form is open.} Four dimensions
constrain the growth of $x_0(d)$ but do not pin its form: the two located
fits refute ratio invariance, yet the absolute-slack and power-law
accounts both remain live at $d_{\mathrm{state}}=96$, where the fit is
$100\%$ bootstrap-degenerate from real per-seed scatter. % evidence: C11
Discriminating them requires either variance reduction at the noisiest
loads or a window that brackets the $d_{\mathrm{state}}=96$ transition
from above, and a registered analysis found the present grid underpowered
for that discrimination; we therefore make no functional-form claim.
% evidence: C13

\paragraph{Near-ceiling fine structure at $d_{\mathrm{state}}=96$ is
instrument-limited.} The unlocked grid's non-monotonic dip-and-recovery
pattern, including the exact-ceiling reading at $K{=}90$, sits close to
the admission instrument's own resolution. A registered two-cell
fresh-seed diagnostic aimed at this fine structure routed to its
pre-registered degenerate outcome: neither cell produced an admissible
reading at the recalibrated gate, and the failing admissibility leg
differed from the one the original grid failed, so the diagnostic's
adjudication triggers were never licensed to fire. % evidence: C14
We therefore scope the $d_{\mathrm{state}}=96$ claim strictly as the
absence of a monotonic collapse through $K/d = 0.9375$, and we treat the
per-load ordering near ceiling as unresolved. The admission gate episode
itself (Section~\ref{sec:frontier}) cuts both ways: it shows the
measurement pipeline can manufacture an apparent collapse, and it is
disclosed precisely so that the reported transitions are read against
that risk; the located $d_{\mathrm{state}} \in \{64, 80\}$ fits pass
their gates without any recalibration.

\paragraph{The dose test bounds the tested variable only.} The
exoneration of Section~\ref{sec:dose} covers scalar coherence dose under
two frozen injection structures (rank-4 and rank-48). It does not cover
coherence that co-evolves with training: a non-frozen
$d_{\mathrm{state}}=128$ reference point whose table coherence drifted to
$[0.127, 0.202]$ under ordinary training also recovers at $h4 = 1.0$,
% evidence: C3
but such a point confounds the coherence level with how the level was
reached, and is reported as context, not as part of the controlled sweep.
Mechanistic accounts below the level of the disclosed comparison axes
(for example, optimization dynamics through the larger eval-time
Newton--Schulz block) are untested here.

\paragraph{One architecture family.} Whether the same three-part
structure, a sharp transition, its dissolution with scale, and a
coherence exoneration, appears in other fast-weight update rules or
state-space families is untested; the measurement recipe (pre-registered
logistic localization, degenerate-fit disclosure, saturation checks,
admission gates) is the transferable artifact.
