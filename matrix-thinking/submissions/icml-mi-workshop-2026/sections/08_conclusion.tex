\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The matrix-bottleneck training objective in CODI does not reward
rank: the readout Jacobian carries no rank information through the
chain rule, the flat rank-$k$ curves are insensitive to nonlinear
readouts that escape the linear-Jacobian shortcut, and three seeds
under matched hyperparameters land at effective ranks
$\{4, 12, 13\}$ with statistically indistinguishable accuracy. The
rank-$k$ probe alone could not distinguish rank-blindness from
position-irrelevance; the seed-level rank spread does.
