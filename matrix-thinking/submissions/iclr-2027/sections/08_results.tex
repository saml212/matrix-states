The pre-spend gate described in Section~\ref{sec:the-fix} predicted
$K16\ h4{=}1.00$ and $K32\ h4{=}0.77$ from measured cross-episode drift
($0.94$ at $K{=}16$, $0.90$ at $K{=}32$): training stabilizes keys
relative to the untrained-probe reference, and this resolved the attack's
cross-episode-drift risk favorably enough to authorize launch. The gate
itself was registered on $K{=}16$ only; the $K{=}32$ prediction was
explicitly non-gating, which matters below as the pre-mortem answer on
how well the simulator was calibrated.

\subsection{K=16: Minimum-Publishable Headline --- Hit}

Every one of 3/3 seeds is admissible. Held-out four-hop recovery reaches
$0.95$--$1.00$ against the pre-registered bar $\geq0.8$ and the baseline
$0.42$--$0.47$; $h{=}7$ reaches $0.55$--$0.67$ against a baseline of
$0.07$--$0.10$; the $h{=}1$ no-sacrifice guard holds at $1.0$ everywhere
(Figure~\ref{fig:fgeo3-headline}, left).

\subsection{K=32: \texorpdfstring{$\sim$45$\times$}{~45x}, Fully
Admissible, Bar Narrowly Missed --- and the Miss Is Attributed}

This is the paper's central honest disclosure, and we report it in the
order the evidence resolved it, not the order that reads best.
\textbf{Admissibility.} The original \texttt{n\_iter=12} cells were 0/3
admissible, disqualified solely on the Newton-Schulz fallback criterion
($56/20{,}000$ steps $=0.28\%$ eigh fallback, per the substitute stack of
Section~\ref{sec:the-fix}). The audit-verified \texttt{n\_iter=20}
escalation completed with all 3 seeds fully admissible --- zero fallback
steps, every substitute-stack criterion passed --- so the improvement
below is claimable at full evidentiary tier: $h{=}4$ moves
$0.009\to0.390/0.416/0.504$, a $\sim$45$\times$ improvement; $h{=}2$
reaches $1.0$ against a baseline of $0.26$; $h{=}3$ reaches
$0.87$--$0.95$ against $0.05$. \textbf{The headline bar
($\geq0.5$) is not met.} $h{=}4 = 0.504/0.416/0.390$, mean $0.44$, with
only $1/3$ seeds at the line. We report this exactly, never rounded up
and never restated as ``approximately met.'' \textbf{The miss is
attributed, not left unexplained.} Trained cross-episode key drift at
$K{=}32$ measures $0.9037$, inside the pre-registered high-drift band
($<0.95$) that an earlier adversarial round named and measured
\emph{before} this wave ran --- the mechanism the design's own outcome-F
row predicted. Within-episode orthogonality is delivered by construction
(residual $\approx0$); cross-episode key \emph{identity} is the residual
requirement, since a per-identity $v_{\mathrm{eff}}$ cannot chase an
episode-conditional key. This is not left as future work: the direct
follow-on --- cross-episode key anchoring --- has since run to completion
across four waves, reported below. A companion finding rules out a
competing account of the miss: the \texttt{n\_iter=20} cells (zero
fallbacks) are numerically identical to the \texttt{n\_iter=12} cells (56
fallbacks), $0.504/0.416/0.390$ vs.\ $0.39$--$0.50$. The 56
``corrupted'' steps changed nothing, which both validates the admission
criterion as conservative rather than explanatory and rules out solver
noise as a competing account of the residual gap.

\subsection{What Does Not Move: h=21}

Literal-depth composition still collapses under F-geo-3: orthogonality
fixes write-time interference between bindings, not the separate,
iteration-compounding mechanism of Section~\ref{sec:phenomenon}'s
depth-amplification law. We name this as the fix's own scope boundary, a
mechanism explicitly scoped out, not a gap in the evidence.

\subsection{The Key-Anchoring Program's Results}

Section~\ref{sec:the-fix} introduces the anchoring follow-on and its
headline; here we report the numbers behind it, in the order the four
waves established them. \textbf{(1) The behavioral gain, independently
replicated.} $K{=}32$ $h{=}4$ recovery lifts from a freshly-measured
baseline of $0.4105$ to $0.61$--$0.71$ across 9 seed-runs spanning 3
distinct seed sets and 2 architecture variants (global-$\lambda$
candidate~(d), per-entity-$\lambda$ candidate~(d$'$)) --- upgraded from
``reproduced'' to ``independently replicated'' because 6 of the 9 runs
used genuinely fresh seeds never drawn anywhere in this program before.
\textbf{(2) The mechanism rejection (Outcome C), confirmed twice.} A
purpose-built per-entity engagement instrument
(\texttt{engaged\_frac\_v3}, median $r_e$) reads under $14$--$41\%$
engagement at every $K{=}32$ leg, both architecture variants, confirmed
across two independent mechanism-tier waves at increasing evidentiary
rigor (unit-anchor-norm measured directly rather than assumed; a formal
dip-test ruling out masked bimodality; an effect-size floor ruling out
power-manufactured engagement) and immune to a specific rescue attempt
(a $\lambda$-scaled threshold re-derivation) that was itself
independently attacked and rejected. \textbf{(3) The actual explanation,
confirmed by ablation.} A frozen, never-trained anchor table (random
init or frame-potential init, both tested) at the same blend weight
matches or exceeds the learned table's own gain ($0.67$--$0.76$ vs.\
$0.61$--$0.71$), with a negative-median $r_e$ reading in the random-init
arm confirming the instrument correctly reports ``nothing to align to.''
This is confirmed by ablation, not merely suggested by it: the
prediction that a frozen table should match the gain if constancy alone
is the channel was registered before the confirming data existed, and it
held. \textbf{(4) The measured capacity boundary.} Extending the
identical design to $K{=}48$ ($K/d{=}0.75$), candidate~(d) is fully
admissible (3/3 seeds) and both of its own named explanatory channels
--- drift-space closure ($\sim$81\% of the gap to ceiling) and value-Gram
relief ($0.626\times$ the reference's deviation) --- are measurably
active, yet the bar is still missed 0/3 seeds (mean $0.0215$ vs.\
$0.024434$). The resulting three-point curve
($\sim1.00\to\sim0.65\to\sim0.02$ at $K/d{=}0.25/0.5/0.75$,
Figure~\ref{fig:capacity-curve}) is a cliff, not a smooth decline, while
the theoretical $\lambda{=}1$ ceiling declines only gently over the same
range ($0.97\to0.94\to0.90$): binding survives everywhere ($h{=}1$ guard
$1.0000$ at every $K$), and it is composition that collapses. This
cliff's precise location, its dissolution at $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}128$,
the direct exoneration of anchor-table coherence as its cause, and the
super-linear (not ratio-invariant) growth of load tolerance with
$d_{\mathrm{state}}$ are a localization program of their own, reported in
full in Section~\ref{sec:phenomenon}'s $K{=}48$ rider
(Section~\ref{sec:k48-rider}) together with the still-open
$d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}96$ non-monotonic reading rather than re-derived
here.

State plainly, as the paper's own honesty discipline requires: the
behavioral $K{=}32$ gain is real and its cause is now known (constancy,
confirmed by ablation); the entity-alignment hypothesis originally
proposed for it is rejected; and the fix's regime is bounded at
$K/d\leq0.5$. This item, open at an earlier stage of this project, is now
closed.

Frame sentence for the section, and for the paper's central demonstration
as a whole: the fix moves the frontier decisively at $K{=}16$ (bar hit,
3/3), moves it $\sim$45$\times$ at $K{=}32$ without clearing the
pre-registered bar, and the residual gap is attributed to a pre-measured
second mechanism whose own follow-on fix is itself now fully
characterized --- a complete causal chain, with the boundary of what
remains open (Section~\ref{sec:discussion}) stated as precisely as the
boundary of what is resolved.
