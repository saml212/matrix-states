\section{The Phenomenon: Rank Recruitment Is Reliable, Exactness Is Not}
\label{sec:phenomenon}

\subsection{Synthetic baseline: the razor cliff}

On the synthetic binding task, an unconstrained DeltaNet layer trained
end-to-end saturates to $\mathrm{rec@0.9}=1.0000$ at every tested $(d,K)$
cell, with no late-transition regime (Wave~0). Recruitment is exact, not
merely sufficient: entity-subspace rank equals whole-matrix rank equals
$K$ to four decimal places (e.g.\ $31.9993$--$31.9994$ of $K{=}32$) --- the
model does not recruit more rank than the task requires. An eval-time
truncation staircase at $d{=}64,K{=}32$ makes this necessity causal rather
than merely observed: recovery sits at ceiling ($1.0000$) for every
$k\geq32$ and every composition depth up to $h{=}21$, and collapses in a
single step below it --- $h{=}1$ falls from $1.0000$ to $0.9681$ at
$k{=}31$, $h{=}21$ from $1.0000$ to $0.3412$ --- matching the theoretical
$(K{-}h)/K$ bimodal per-mode-drop prediction to within $0.001$--$0.003$ at
every hop tested.

\subsection{Real-data transfer: the graded frontier}

Transplanting the identical architecture, delta rule, and provable bound
onto GPT-2-tokenized language reproduces the necessity result but changes
its shape entirely (Figure~\ref{fig:trunc-synth-real}). The eval-truncation
staircase is still causal --- recovery reaches its ceiling exactly at
$k{=}K$, for every $K$ tested --- but that ceiling is graded, not
razor-sharp, and it degrades with $K$: the transition window with
non-trivial sub-ceiling recovery widens from 2--3 ranks at $K{=}8$ to
12--13 ranks at $K{=}32$, the single steepest jump shrinks from $+0.375$ to
$+0.066$, and the ceiling itself falls from $1.000$ to $0.995$ to $0.938$
to $0.795$ across $K\in\{8,16,24,32\}$. On real data the transition is a
graded window several ranks wide, never a single-step threshold --- a
distinction worth stating explicitly, since it is easy to misquote as a
razor cliff, and that is not what was measured here.

This graded collapse is not a rank-recruitment failure
(Figure~\ref{fig:rank-recruited}): entity-subspace rank tracks
$94$--$99\%$ of target $K$ at every tested load ($99\%$ at $K{=}8$, $98\%$
at $K{=}16$, $96$--$97\%$ at $K{=}24$, $94\%$ at $K{=}32$), essentially
unchanged from the synthetic case. What degrades instead is per-binding
write quality within the recruited rank
(Figure~\ref{fig:k-axis-frontier}): in-distribution recovery
($h{=}1/2/3$) and held-out recovery ($h{=}4$--$7$) both fall steeply with
$K$ --- from $1.000/0.999/0.978$ ($0.944/0.873/0.780/0.699$ held out) at
$K{=}8$, through $0.997/0.90/0.68$
($0.42$--$0.47/0.25$--$0.28/0.14$--$0.16/0.07$--$0.10$) at $K{=}16$ and
$0.94/0.58/0.27$ ($0.10/0.03/0.01/0.00$) at $K{=}24$, to $0.78/0.26/0.05$
($0.009/0.001/0.000/0.000$) at $K{=}32$.

\subsection{The depth-amplification law}

Raw composition depth is a sharper exactness probe than any single-hop
cosine tolerance: a rank-$(K{-}1)$ operator passes shallow hops (at
$\sqrt{1-1/K}$ per-dropped-mode cosine) and only reveals its deficiency at
depth, an $\varepsilon^h$-shaped decay that recurs three more times in
this paper --- in the soft-fix interventions' proportional but
insufficient gains (Section~\ref{sec:soft-fixes}), in the fix's own
unrepaired $h{=}21$ residual (Section~\ref{sec:results}), and in why the
capacity curve below is a cliff rather than a smooth decline. This decay
curve is a function of $K$ alone: it is invariant to hop supervision
(training directly at $h{=}4,5$ produces recovery statistically
indistinguishable from held-out extrapolation --- e.g.\ $K{=}16$, $h{=}4$:
$0.390$--$0.503$ trained-at vs.\ $0.419$--$0.465$ extrapolated) and to a
$2.5\times$ larger training budget ($62.5$K vs.\ $25$K steps moves nothing
at any $K$). One caveat travels with $h{=}21$ everywhere it recurs: it is
a depth-decay probe on raw iteration count, not an independent
held-out-hop test, since $21\bmod K$ collides with in-distribution
residues at small $K$; we read it via its effective hop throughout.

\subsection{The K=48 rider: the frontier breaks past $K\approx d/2$, and
the break is now a located cliff}
\label{sec:k48-rider}

The frontier does not merely degrade past $K{=}d/2$ --- it breaks. On the
unfixed baseline, key-Gram deviation keeps growing past $K{=}32$
($2.7\to4.3$ at $K{=}48$), $h{=}1$ recovery halves ($0.41$--$0.44$), and
composition is gone entirely by $h\geq2$ ($0.005$--$0.009$); the surgical
orthonormal-key pin correctly refuses to apply here, by its own
dimensional guard ($96$ train-plus-held-out identity vectors exceed
$d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}64$). This is no longer only an observed breakdown:
the fix's own operating regime, extended to $K{=}48$ ($K/d{=}0.75$) in
Section~\ref{sec:the-fix}, is fully admissible (3/3 seeds) but misses its
own pre-registered bar 0/3 seeds (mean $0.0215$ vs.\ bar $0.024434$), with
both of the design's own explanatory channels --- drift-space closure
($\sim$81\% of the gap to ceiling) and value-Gram relief ($0.626\times$
the reference's deviation) --- measurably active yet insufficient. The
resulting three-point curve ($\sim1.00$ at $K{=}16\to\sim0.65$ at
$K{=}32\to\sim0.02$ at $K{=}48$) falls off a cliff between half and
three-quarter capacity, while the theoretical $\lambda{=}1$ ceiling
declines only gently over the same range ($0.97\to0.94\to0.90$): binding
survives everywhere ($h{=}1$ guard $1.0000$, every cell, every $K$), and
it is composition that collapses.

A dedicated localization wave sharpens this from a bracket to a point
estimate (Figure~\ref{fig:cliff}, left panel). Measuring four new loads
--- $K{=}34,38,42,46$ ($K/d{=}0.53125,0.59375,0.65625,0.71875$), 3 seeds
each --- alongside the three archived anchors gives a 7-point curve, to
which a sigmoid $h_4(x)=L/(1+\exp((x-x_0)/w))$ fits with a $4{,}000$-trial
seed-level parametric bootstrap: the transition lands at $x_0{=}0.5455$
(95\% CI $[0.5385,0.5513]$, width $0.0127$, 0/4000 degenerate fits) with
width $w{=}0.0597$ (CI $[0.0557,0.0642]$) --- an approximately $20\times$
tightening of the prior 0.25-wide bracket. This localizes \emph{where} and
\emph{how wide} the transition is at a single $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}64$;
it makes no mechanism claim, and covers the candidate-fix arm only (a
disclosed budget tradeoff left the reference arm unmeasured at the four
new loads).

Whether that location is a universal property of the ratio $K/d$, or an
artifact of $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}64$ specifically, has a clear and
negative answer (Figure~\ref{fig:cliff}, right panel). Re-running the
identical $K/d$ window at $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}128$
($K{=}68,76,84,92$) shows $h4{=}1.0$ at every load, every seed, 12/12
cells --- the transition does not shift, it exits the measured window
entirely (instrument saturation is ruled out: hop-21 recovery is not
uniformly $1.0$, and effective rank tracks each $K$ almost exactly).

\subsection{The leading confound, tested directly: coherence-dose
response}
\label{sec:dose-response}

The two waves above differ in more than raw scale. The $d{=}64$ anchor
table (107 entities in 64 dimensions) is forced non-orthogonal by the
Welch bound (construction-time $\max|\cos|{=}0.2842$, drifting under
training to a final-checkpoint range of $0.373$--$0.385$); the $d{=}128$
table (107 entities, 128 dimensions) is exactly orthogonal at construction
($\max|\cos|{=}0.000000$). This is the single most natural mechanistic
candidate for the cliff's disappearance, and we test it directly rather
than by inference: holding $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}128$, $n{=}107$, $K{=}68$
fixed at exactly the geometry measured flat above, we inject controlled,
\emph{frozen} coherence into the anchor table and sweep the dose
$\{0.130,0.284,0.40\}$ --- the last exceeding $d{=}64$'s own realized
training band --- under both a concentrated (rank-4) and a diffuse
($\mathrm{subspace\_rank}{=}48$, the maximum scanned rank whose achieved
dose ceiling still reaches $0.40$) injection structure. The frozen table's
gradient path is verified severed at launch, and its coherence verified
bit-identical across every recorded checkpoint at harvest.

Both structures come back flat (Figure~\ref{fig:dose}): $h4{=}1.0$ at
every cell, every dose, for both the rank-4 arm (10/10 cells) and the
diffuse arm (9/9 cells), including at dose $0.40$. This is the strongest
exoneration this design can produce, since a diffuse, maximum-scanned-rank
injection was the one structural escape hatch a rank-4-only result would
have left open: coherence is exonerated at both the concentrated and the
diffuse structure, closing the two-structure requirement this program set
for itself before either wave ran. Neither scalar nor structural
coherence reproduces the $d{=}64$ cliff at matched
$K/d_{\mathrm{state}}/n$ geometry (instrument saturation is again ruled
out: hop-21 recovery ranges $0.9963$--$1.0$ across all cells, not
uniformly $1.0$). With coherence fully excluded, the one candidate this
program's own design left untested is raw state capacity:
$d_{\mathrm{state}}$ grew $4\times$ between the cliff's location and its
disappearance while $K$ only grew $\sim2\times$ at the matched ratio.

We then test that candidate directly. Re-running the same localization
procedure at $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}80$ places the transition at
$x_0{=}0.6779$ (95\% CI $[0.6683,0.6867]$, clean, non-degenerate,
$n{=}5$ seeds/$K$) --- entirely above the pre-registered ratio-invariant
band $[0.4745,0.6165]$ centered on $d{=}64$'s own $x_0{=}0.5455$, a real,
disclosed exclusion of ``the cliff sits at a fixed $K/d$ fraction.'' Load
tolerance grows \emph{super-linearly} in $d_{\mathrm{state}}$, not at a
fixed ratio. At $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}96$, however, a full unlocked grid
($K\in\{69,72,78,84,90\}$, $n{=}3$ seeds/$K$, after an
independently-audited repro instrument diagnosed an initial admissibility
failure on 11/12 new cells as a tolerance miscalibration in the
convergence gate rather than a real capacity wall --- every flagged
episode resolves by $n_{\mathrm{iter}}\leq28$ against a production
setting of $20$, and only the admission flag, never the $h4$ measurement,
was recalibrated) finds no cliff anywhere through $K/d{=}0.9375$, but a
non-monotonic curve ($0.9592/0.9216/0.9326/0.9581/1.0000$ across the five
loads) that a sigmoid cannot localize: 100\% of $4{,}000$ bootstrap
resamples land at the fit's own degenerate bounds. Neither of the two
simplest rival functional forms for the super-linear growth --- an
absolute-slack account ($x_0\propto d_{\mathrm{state}}$ minus a constant,
band $[0.718,0.739]$) nor a power-law account (band $[0.768,0.837]$) ---
is confirmed or excluded by this data; a follow-up pool-margin
diagnostic, designed to test whether $K{=}84$'s dip below $K{=}90$
reflects a genuine non-monotonicity, instead returned degenerate readings
at fresh, independent seeds and is reported as an open
instrument-sensitivity finding (the admission frontier itself moves with
$K/d$, and a real but not-yet-isolated pool-margin effect was observed at
the inadmissible cells) rather than a clean adjudication --- the full
per-cell trail is reported in Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix-tables}. The
precise functional form of the growth, and the exact
$d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}96$ transition point, remain open; seed escalation
at the noisiest measured group ($K{=}72$, per-seed range
$0.8426$--$0.9904$) is the registered next step, not launched by this
paper's own evidence base.

\subsection{Where this line of inquiry started}

The $K{=}32$ operating point was not chosen after the fact. In the
earliest wave of this program, the unconstrained baseline's own $K{=}32$
rerun plateaus at $\mathrm{rec@0.9}=0.78$--$0.80$ for the final
$21{,}000$ of $25{,}000$ training steps --- flat, not still climbing, with
entity-subspace rank $29.9$--$30.2$ confirming capacity was never the
issue even then --- while the $K{=}16$ arm converges cleanly to
$0.996$--$0.999$ over the same run. This single anomalous plateau is what
designated $K{=}32$ the exactness-mechanism program's headline cell
throughout the rest of this paper.
