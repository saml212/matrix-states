\subsection{Synthetic baseline: the razor cliff}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Wave 0 (\texttt{DELTANET\_CAUSAL\_RANK\_DESIGN.md} \S12.3):
    unconstrained arm saturates $\mathrm{rec@0.9}{=}1.0000$ at EVERY tested
    $(d,K)$ cell, no late-transition regime.
  \item No inflation (\S12.4): entity-subspace rank $=$ whole-matrix rank
    $=K$ to 4 decimals (e.g.\ $31.9993$--$31.9994$ of $K{=}32$).
  \item Eval-truncation staircase (\S12.8.3), $d{=}64,K{=}32$: ceiling
    $k{\geq}32 \Rightarrow 1.0000$ at every $h$ incl.\ $h{=}21$; cliff
    $k{=}31{\to}32$: $h{=}1$ $0.9681{\to}1.0000$, $h{=}21$
    $0.3412{\to}1.0000$. Matches the $(K{-}h)/K$ bimodal per-mode-drop
    theory to $0.001$--$0.003$ at every hop.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Real-data transfer: the graded frontier (headline, Fig.~2)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Same architecture, same delta rule, same provable bound,
    transplanted to GPT-2-tokenized language.
  \item $\mathrm{rec@0.9}$, ID $h{=}1/2/3$ (held-out $h{=}4$--$7$ in
    parens): $K{=}8$: $1.000/0.999/0.978$ ($0.944/0.873/0.780/0.699$);
    $K{=}16$: $0.997/0.90/0.68$ ($0.42$--$0.47/0.25$--$0.28/0.14$--$0.16/
    0.07$--$0.10$); $K{=}24$: $0.94/0.58/0.27$
    ($0.10/0.03/0.01/0.00$); $K{=}32$: $0.78/0.26/0.05$
    ($0.009/0.001/0.000/0.000$).
  \item Rank recruitment intact regardless (Fig.~3): entity-subspace rank
    99\% ($K{=}8$), 98\% ($K{=}16$), 96--97\% ($K{=}24$), 94\% ($K{=}32$)
    of target.
  \item Eval-truncation staircase is causal (RD-1 CONFIRMED) but graded:
    ceiling reached EXACTLY at $k{=}K$ at every $K$ --- but the ceiling
    ITSELF falls with $K$: $1.000 \to 0.995 \to 0.938 \to 0.795$ at
    $K{=}8/16/24/32$. Window below ceiling widens: 2--3 ranks ($K{=}8$)
    $\to$ 12--13 ranks ($K{=}32$); steepest single-step jump shrinks
    $+0.375 \to +0.066$.
  \item \textbf{Mandatory qualifier, verbatim from source:} ``the
    real-data transition is graded across a window several ranks wide,
    not a single-step threshold ... If a future write-up quotes `razor
    cliff at $k{=}K{-}1\to K$' for the real-data result, that is not what
    was measured here.''
\end{itemize}

\subsection{The depth-amplification law}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Imported from \texttt{TASK\_E\_FINDINGS.md} \S3: raw
    iteration/composition depth is a sharper exactness probe than
    single-hop cosine tolerance. Rank-$(K{-}1)$ operator passes shallow
    hops ($\sqrt{1{-}1/K}$ per-dropped-mode cosine) and only reveals
    deficiency at depth.
  \item Theoretical spine for ``graded at $h{=}1$'' $\to$ ``collapsed by
    $h{=}4$'': an $\varepsilon^h$-shaped law, recurs 3 more times (Wave
    F's proportionality reading \S\ref{sec:soft-fixes}; the K=48 rider
    below; the fix's own $h{=}21$ residual failure
    \S\ref{sec:results}).
  \item Deephop program CLOSED (\texttt{DELTANET\_REALDATA\_DESIGN.md}
    \S18.1): decay curve is a function of $K$ ALONE --- invariant to hop
    supervision (training AT $h{=}4,5$ produces statistically
    indistinguishable recovery from held-out extrapolation, e.g.\ $K{=}16$
    $h{=}4$: $0.390$--$0.503$ trained-at vs.\ $0.419$--$0.465$
    extrapolated) AND to 2.5$\times$ training budget (62.5K vs.\ 25K steps
    moves nothing at any $K$).
  \item $h{=}21$ caveat, cite every time it recurs: a DEPTH-DECAY PROBE
    (raw iteration count), not an independent held-out-hop test --- $21
    \bmod K$ collides with in-distribution residues at small $K$; read via
    \texttt{effective\_hop}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{K=48 rider: the frontier breaks past $K \approx d/2$ --- now a
measured cliff, not an open question (\S\ref{sec:the-fix}, \S\ref{sec:results},
Fig.~11)}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Key-Gram deviation keeps growing ($2.7$ at $K{=}32 \to 4.3$ at
    $K{=}48$); $h{=}1$ recovery halves ($0.41$--$0.44$); composition gone
    by $h{\geq}2$ ($0.005$--$0.009$) \textbf{on the unfixed baseline}.
  \item i-strong pin correctly REFUSED here by its own dimensional guard
    (96 train+held-out identity vectors $> d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}64$).
  \item \textbf{Updated status: this is no longer only an observed
    breakdown of the unfixed baseline --- the fix's own operating regime
    was extended to this cell and completed as a measured cliff
    (\S\ref{sec:the-fix}).} At $K{=}48$ ($K/d{=}0.75$), F-geo-3 plus the
    key-anchoring follow-on is fully admissible (3/3 seeds) but misses its
    own pre-registered bar 0/3 seeds (mean $0.0215$ vs.\ bar $0.024434$),
    with both of the design's own explanatory channels (drift-space
    closure, $\sim$81\% of the gap; value-Gram relief, $0.626\times$ the
    reference) measurably active yet insufficient. The three-point curve
    ($\sim$1.00 at $K{=}16 \to \sim$0.65 at $K{=}32 \to \sim$0.02 at
    $K{=}48$) is a cliff between half and three-quarter capacity, while the
    theoretical $\lambda{=}1$ ceiling declines only gently over the same
    range ($0.97 \to 0.94 \to 0.90$) --- the shortfall is a real,
    structural saturation, not a measurement artifact. Binding survives
    everywhere ($h{=}1$ guard $1.0000$, every cell, every $K$); composition
    is what collapses.
  \item What remains genuinely open (see limitations): \emph{why} the
    break is so sharp between $K/d{=}0.5$ and $K/d{=}0.75$ when the
    ceiling itself declines gently --- the design's own named, untested
    candidate is value-crowding (more entities packed into the same fixed
    value dimensionality), which would require a fresh design to test.
  \item \textbf{The cliff is now LOCATED, not merely bracketed
    (Fig.~\ref{fig:cliff}).} A dedicated localization wave measured 4 new
    $K$'s --- $34, 38, 42, 46$ ($K/d{=}0.53125/0.59375/0.65625/0.71875$),
    3 seeds each --- alongside the 3 archived anchors, and fit a sigmoid
    $h_4(x) {=} L/(1{+}\exp((x{-}x_0)/w))$ to the resulting 7-point curve
    with a 4,000-trial seed-level parametric bootstrap CI. The transition
    lands at $x_0{=}0.5455$ (95\% CI $[0.5385, 0.5513]$, width $0.0127$,
    0/4000 degenerate fits), $w{=}0.0597$ (CI $[0.0557, 0.0642]$) ---
    narrowing the cliff's location from the prior 0.25-wide $(0.5, 0.75)$
    bracket to a $\pm0.0064$-wide point estimate, a $\approx$20$\times$
    tightening, without needing to resolve the sigmoid-vs-linear shape
    question (retracted as underpowered at 6 points before any new data was
    collected). \textbf{Scope, stated precisely:} candidate-(d) arm only
    (no fresh reference arm at the 4 new $K$'s, a disclosed budget
    tradeoff); no mechanism claim (this locates \emph{where} and \emph{how
    wide} the transition is, not \emph{why} it occurs at this $K/d$ ratio);
    measured at a single $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}64$.
  \item \textbf{The universality question above is now ANSWERED, and the
    answer is negative (Fig.~\ref{fig:cliff}, right panel): the cliff is
    dimension-dependent, not a universal property of $K/d$.} Re-running
    the identical $K/d$ window at $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}128$
    ($K{=}68,76,84,92$, the same four ratios) shows $h4{=}1.0$ at every
    load, every seed, 12/12 cells --- the transition does not shift, it
    exits the measured window entirely. Instrument saturation is ruled out
    (hop-21 recovery is not uniformly $1.0$; effective rank tracks each
    $K$ almost exactly). Full mechanism testing --- whether this tracks
    anchor-table coherence, forced by the Welch bound only when entity
    count exceeds $d_{\mathrm{state}}$ --- is reported next
    (\S\ref{sec:dose-response}).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{The leading confound, tested directly: coherence-dose response}
\label{sec:dose-response}
\begin{itemize}
  \item The two waves above (Fig.~\ref{fig:cliff}) differ in more than raw
    scale: the $d{=}64$ anchor table (107 entities packed into 64
    dimensions) is forced non-orthogonal by the Welch bound (measured
    coherence $\max|\cos|{=}0.2842$ at construction, drifting under normal
    training to a final-checkpoint range-of-$K$-means of
    $0.373$--$0.385$); the $d{=}128$ table (107 entities, 128 dimensions)
    is exactly orthogonal at construction ($\max|\cos|{=}0.000000$). This
    is the single most natural remaining mechanistic candidate for the
    cliff's disappearance, and we test it directly rather than by
    inference.
  \item \textbf{Design.} Hold $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}128$, $n{=}107$,
    $K{=}68$ fixed at exactly the geometry measured flat above; inject
    controlled, \emph{frozen} coherence into the anchor table under a
    concentrated (rank-4) injection structure; sweep the dose
    $\{0.130, 0.284, 0.40\}$, the last exceeding $d{=}64$'s own realized
    training band. The frozen table's gradient path is verified severed at
    launch (\texttt{anchor\_table.weight.grad is None}) and its coherence
    verified bit-identical across all 10 recorded checkpoints per cell at
    harvest --- zero drift, zero tolerance slack used.
  \item \textbf{Result: $h4{=}1.0$ at every cell, every dose, 10/10, no
    exception} (Fig.~\ref{fig:dose}) --- flat across the entire dose
    range, including $0.40$. This is the strongest possible exoneration
    this design can produce for the rank-4 structure: directly-injected
    anchor-table coherence, frozen for the full run and verified never to
    drift, does not reproduce the $d{=}64$ cliff at matched
    $K/d_{\mathrm{state}}/n$ geometry. Instrument saturation is again ruled
    out (hop-21 recovery ranges $0.9987$--$1.0$ across all 10 cells, not
    uniformly $1.0$).
  \item \textbf{Scope, stated precisely:} this is a rank-4 (concentrated)
    injection only --- it cannot distinguish ``coherence-as-a-scalar does
    not drive the cliff'' from ``coherence-of-this-particular-structural
    -kind does not drive it, but a diffuse ($\mathrm{subspace\_rank}{=}48$)
    injection might.'' The diffuse co-primary arm is registered but
    PI-gated, not run by this wave. Neither of the two most naturally
    testable single-variable accounts of the cliff's dissolution ($K/d$
    ratio; scalar rank-4 coherence) is sufficient; overlap structure and
    raw state capacity ($d_{\mathrm{state}}$ grew $4\times$ while $K$ only
    grew $\sim$2$\times$ across the two waves, a confound this design
    cannot separate) remain the two live, unadjudicated candidates
    (\S\ref{sec:discussion}).
  \item \textbf{The raw-state-capacity candidate is then tested directly,
    at two further $d_{\mathrm{state}}$'s, and load tolerance grows
    SUPER-LINEARLY rather than staying fixed in $K/d$.} Re-running the
    same localization fit used to place the $d{=}64$ cliff
    (\S\ref{sec:phenomenon}'s K=48 rider above) at $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}80$
    locates its transition at $x_0{=}0.6779$ (95\% CI $[0.6683,0.6867]$,
    clean, non-degenerate, $n{=}5$ seeds/$K$) --- entirely above the
    pre-registered ratio-invariant band $[0.4745,0.6165]$ centered on
    $d{=}64$'s own $x_0{=}0.5455$, a real, disclosed exclusion of ``the
    cliff sits at a fixed $K/d$ fraction.'' \textbf{At
    $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}96$, a full unlocked grid ($K{\in}\{69,72,78,84,90\}$,
    $n{=}3$ seeds/$K$) finds NO cliff anywhere through $K/d{=}0.9375$, but
    the curve is non-monotonic rather than flat-at-ceiling:} per-$K$ means
    $0.9592/0.9216/0.9326/0.9581/1.0000$ dip through $K{=}72/78$, partially
    recover at $K{=}84$, and hit an exact ceiling at $K{=}90$. (11 of the
    12 new cells were initially flagged inadmissible by the convergence
    gate; an independently-audited repro instrument diagnosed this as a
    tolerance miscalibration --- all 4,608 flagged episodes resolve by
    $n_{\mathrm{iter}}{\leq}28$ against a production $n_{\mathrm{iter}}{=}20$
    --- and the admission flag alone, never the $h4$ measurement, was
    recalibrated, unlocking the grid at zero new GPU spend.) The sigmoid
    fit on the unlocked grid is degenerate at its own bounds
    ($x_0{=}0.9$, $L{=}1.2$), with 100\% of 4,000 bootstrap resamples
    degenerate --- \textbf{$x_0(96)$ does not localize}, from genuine
    per-seed scatter (the noisiest group, $K{=}72$, spans
    $0.8426$--$0.9904$) rather than the data gap that blocked the earlier
    narrow-grid attempt. An absolute-slack account
    ($x_0 \propto d_{\mathrm{state}}$ minus a constant, band
    $[0.718,0.739]$) and a power-law account (band $[0.768,0.837]$) are
    both still live and neither is excluded --- \textbf{the precise
    functional form of the super-linear growth is not yet resolved, and
    neither is the exact $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}96$ transition point.} Seed
    escalation at $K{=}72$ (the noisiest group) is the registered next
    step, not launched by this wave.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Connective tissue: where this whole research line started}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Wave 0's K=32 rerun plateaus at $\mathrm{rec@0.9}$ $0.78$--$0.80$
    for the final 21{,}000 of 25{,}000 steps (flat, not climbing;
    entity-subspace rank $29.9$--$30.2$ --- capacity NOT the issue even
    then) while K=16 converges cleanly to $0.996$--$0.999$. This single
    anomalous plateau motivated designating $K{=}32$ the
    exactness-mechanism program's headline cell.
\end{itemize}
