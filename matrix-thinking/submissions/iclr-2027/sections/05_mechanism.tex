\subsection{Design}

Section~\ref{sec:phenomenon} establishes that per-binding exactness, not
recruited rank, is what degrades with $K$ on real text. To find the cause
we hold grammar, model, and loss fixed and vary only the embedding source
feeding the write-time key $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$: learned (the baseline,
arm~iii), frozen orthonormal (arm~i), frozen real GPT-2 span embeddings
(arm~ii), and a Gram-matched frozen non-orthonormal control (arm~iv). The
resulting claim tier is causal but premise-conditional, and the two
comparisons it licenses are not the same claim: frozen-vs-learned
(arms~i/ii/iv vs.\ iii) bundles geometry \emph{and} trainable-parameter
count together, while arm~i vs.\ arm~iv is the geometry-only contrast,
conditional on arm~iv's own re-verification gate.

\subsection{Result: Every Geometry Converges to the Same Attractor}

Every one of arms~i, ii, and iv converges to the learned baseline's own
$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ geometry: key-Gram deviation lands at $2.71$--$2.92$ at
$K{=}32$ against the learned arm's own $2.71$--$2.77$
(Figure~\ref{fig:embedding-attractor}). Raw embedding geometry is
therefore causally irrelevant to the write-time key population that
forms --- the trained $W_k$/convolution path maps every tested input
geometry onto the same non-orthonormal write-geometry basin, regardless
of what geometry it started from.

\subsection{Complementary Evidence: Measured-\texorpdfstring{$\beta$}{beta}
Reconstruction}

A closed-form crosstalk reconstruction using the measured, per-run
$\beta$ (rather than the idealized $\beta{=}1$) leaves a small residual:
$0.0038$ at $K{=}16$ and $0.0041$ at $K{=}32$, against $0.148$/$0.377$
under the wrong-regime $\beta{=}1$ idealization. ``Geometry $+\,\beta$''
is thus essentially the complete state-formation account, but only at the
weaker, non-causal explanatory tier: the idealization-adequacy test earns
no explanatory claim on its own, while the predicted-vs-measured frontier
match (e.g.\ $K{=}32$: $0.726$ predicted vs.\ $0.789$ measured) earns
``structural, deterministic reproduction'' --- explicitly not a causal
claim, and we hold that distinction crisply rather than let it blur.

\subsection{The Existence Proof: i-strong}

A non-trainable, hand-built, per-identity $k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ pin that
bypasses the learned $W_k$/convolution path entirely reaches key-Gram
deviation $0.000$ and $\mathrm{rec@0.9}{=}1.00/1.00/1.00$ at
$h{=}1/2/3$, $K{=}32$ --- matching the synthetic harness exactly. This
result is a diagnostic tie-breaker, never a standalone causal headline,
because it is two-variable by design (embedding source and projection
bypass are bundled together). But as a tie-breaker it is the paper's own
turning point: from ``here is a gap'' to ``here is what would close it.''
The architecture and the delta rule's own algebra can represent and use
the perfect solution; ordinary training does not find it.

\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$2\times2$}{2x2} Existence Proof:
Orthogonality and Stability Are Each Necessary, Jointly Sufficient}

Cutting the same archive along two independent geometric axes ---
orthogonal vs.\ not, stable (context-free) vs.\ not (episode-conditional)
--- at $K{=}32$ gives a $2\times2$ grid (Figure~\ref{fig:existence-2x2}).
Stable-and-orthogonal (i-strong, pool-restricted to
$32{\leq}d_{\mathrm{state}}$) reaches $h{=}4$ recovery $1.0000$ and
$h{=}7$ recovery $1.0000$. Stable-but-not-orthogonal (the frozen-embedding
arms i/iv) manages only $h{=}4$ $0.0075$--$0.0114$ and $h{=}7$
$\approx0.0000$. Not-stable-but-orthogonal (bare geo3 alone, introduced
in Section~\ref{sec:the-fix}) reaches $h{=}4$ mean $0.44$ and $h{=}7$
mean $0.018$. Not-stable-and-not-orthogonal (the fully-learned baseline)
manages $h{=}4$ $0.009$ and $h{=}7$ $\approx0.0000$. Neither axis alone
clears the $\sim$0.01 floor: orthogonality alone recovers most of the gap
($44$--$56\times$) but plateaus at $0.44$; adding stability on top is not
a partial improvement over that $0.44$, it is a discontinuous jump to
$1.00$. It is this interaction, not either main effect, that the figure
exists to show.

\subsection{Whether SGD Can Reach the Jointly-Sufficient Corner Itself}

Until this point, the ``jointly sufficient'' corner of
Figure~\ref{fig:existence-2x2} was demonstrated only by the hand-built
i-strong pin. The natural next question --- can a \emph{learned}
mechanism reach that corner via SGD? --- is not left open here: we test
it to completion across four waves in Sections~\ref{sec:the-fix} and
\ref{sec:results}, and the short answer is no, not via the hypothesized
channel, but the program found something arguably more interesting. A
learned, per-entity cross-episode key anchor does lift $K{=}32$ $h{=}4$
recovery substantially and reproducibly; the entity-alignment mechanism
hypothesized to explain that gain is rejected by a purpose-built
engagement instrument; the actual explanation, confirmed by a
pre-registered ablation, is that constancy in the blend arithmetic alone
suffices, with no learned entity-specific content required; and this
alternate mechanism's own operating regime is bounded, working at
$K/d\leq0.5$ and failing at $K/d{=}0.75$. We state this precisely rather
than as a substitution: the $2\times2$'s jointly-sufficient corner still
rests on the hand-built i-strong pin for a \emph{causal} demonstration at
$K{=}32$, while the \emph{practical}, SGD-adjacent route tested to reach
that corner does not use the hypothesized mechanism at all and works via
a simpler, constancy-only route instead. Both facts belong in the paper,
kept separate, neither substituting for the other; the full arc, with its
numbers, is in Sections~\ref{sec:the-fix} and \ref{sec:results}.
