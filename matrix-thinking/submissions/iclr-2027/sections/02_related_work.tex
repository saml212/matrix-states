\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Descriptive rank measurement (the primary foil).}
    Nazari \& Rusch (arXiv:2602.04852); Sun et al. (arXiv:2602.02195).
    Same effective-rank instrument, uncontrolled $K$, no necessity
    theorem, no causal intervention. We are the mirror image: controlled
    $K$, provable LOWER bound, causal (train-time + eval-time)
    intervention, on the exact architecture family they measure.
  \item \textbf{Nichani, Lee \& Bietti (arXiv:2412.06538, ICLR 2025
    Spotlight).} Closest prior theoretical construction --- rank-$m$
    existence proof under DISCRETE ARGMAX decoding (rank-1 recovers
    $\approx d$ associations under argmax). Motivates why our readout is
    pinned to exact-continuous cosine recovery, never argmax.
  \item \textbf{Schlag, Irie \& Schmidhuber (arXiv:2102.11174, ICML 2021).}
    Origin of ``linear-attention state as associative memory'' ---
    fast-weight-programmer lineage.
  \item \textbf{DeltaNet / Gated DeltaNet production lineage
    (\texttt{fla-org} kernel used directly) and DeltaProduct
    (arXiv:2502.10297).} DeltaProduct's ``rank'' is the PER-STEP
    TRANSITION matrix $A_t = I - \beta_t k_t k_t^\top$ --- a DIFFERENT
    object from the STATE's own rank $\mathrm{rank}(S_t)$ measured here.
    State this distinction in one explicit sentence (flagged in the source
    design doc as the single most likely reviewer confusion). Also:
    Grazzi et al.\ (arXiv:2411.12537, negative-eigenvalue state tracking)
    for the eigenvalue sign/phase framing relevant to the $h{=}21$
    depth-amplification discussion.
  \item \textbf{Barnfield et al.\ (arXiv:2605.05189).} Capacity thresholds
    for STATIC rank-constrained associative memories (no recurrence, no
    training dynamics). Our delta: the training-time force-rank ablation
    on a time-varying, streamed state, plus held-out-hop composition.
  \item \textbf{Based / Arora et al.\ (arXiv:2402.18668, Thm F.1) and
    Zoology (arXiv:2312.04927).} Based bounds state SIZE
    (decoding-agnostic); justify explicitly why RANK, not size, is the
    binding resource under a pinned linear-unbind readout. Zoology: the
    standard synthetic associative-recall (MQAR) benchmark family our
    probe grammar resembles.
  \item \textbf{M\textsuperscript{2}RNN (arXiv:2603.14360).} Closest
    prior-art paper --- matrix-valued recurrent state, permutation-group
    (S$_3$) composition trained at seq-len 128, evaluated near-perfectly
    at 512. Their own comparison table has a plain vector-state GRU
    matching the matrix model (rank not shown load-bearing); Thm 1 is
    representational, not necessity; no causal rank intervention. Lead
    with what their own results concede, then state the three-way mirror.
    \textbf{VERIFY CITATION SPECIFICS before camera-ready} --- flagged in
    source material as abstract-level-only verification so far.
  \item \textbf{Superposition / distributed representation literature.}
    Elhage et al.\ (Toy Models of Superposition) for the general framing;
    contrast with our EXACT-recovery requirement (superposition results
    typically assume favorable/sparse readout, which our provable bound
    closes off). Plate 1995 (Holographic Reduced Representations), Frady,
    Kleyko \& Sommer 2020 (Resonator Networks) --- VSA/HRR capacity
    tradition, scope explicitly as a DIFFERENT notion of capacity
    (SNR/bundling) from this paper's exact-rank framing.
  \item \textbf{This project's own prior chapter (Task D/E,
    \texttt{matrix-thinking/chapter2/}).} Not external work --- immediate
    lineage. The from-scratch, bespoke-attention-encoder version of the
    identical rank-recruitment result, including the depth-amplification
    law imported directly (\texttt{TASK\_E\_FINDINGS.md} \S3) and the
    earlier ProsQA/matrix-CODI negative result that motivated the
    exact-continuous readout choice in the first place. State explicitly:
    this paper TRANSFERS that result to a production architecture and, new
    here, discovers and fixes the exactness gap the synthetic construction
    never exposed.
\end{itemize}
