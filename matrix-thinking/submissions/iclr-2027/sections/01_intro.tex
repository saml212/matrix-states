\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A recent wave of work measures the rank of the fixed-size state maintained
by fast-weight linear-attention architectures on real, pretrained language
models, and finds it bounded by sequence length \citep{nazari2026rank,
sun2026staterank}. What this literature does not establish is whether
stochastic gradient descent is \emph{forced} to use the rank a task
requires, or whether the resulting linear operator is \emph{exact} once
that rank is recruited. We close both gaps in a single architecture
family, using a production DeltaNet layer (\texttt{fla-org}'s
\texttt{chunk\_delta\_rule} kernel) as the vehicle throughout.

Our first contribution is a provable necessity result: under a pinned
linear-unbind readout (Section~\ref{sec:setup}), exact recovery of $K$
independent bindings from a fast-weight state $S_T$ requires
$\mathrm{rank}(S_T)\geq K$. We confirm this causally, not merely
representationally, via an eval-time truncation staircase on synthetic
data: at $d{=}64$, $K{=}32$, truncating a trained state from rank
$k{=}31$ to $k{=}32$ moves recovery from $0.9681$ to $1.0000$ at
composition depth $h{=}1$, and from $0.3412$ to $1.0000$ at $h{=}21$ --- a
razor-sharp cliff at exactly $k{=}K{-}1{\to}K$, with no inflation
(entity-subspace rank equals whole-matrix rank equals $K$ to four decimal
places). Our second contribution transplants the identical $(d,K)$
operating point onto real, GPT-2-tokenized language and finds the same
causal necessity result holds (RD-1) --- with one mandatory qualifier: the
transition is \emph{graded}, not razor-sharp. The truncation ceiling is
reached exactly at $k{=}K$ at every $K\in\{8,16,24,32\}$ tested, but the
window below that ceiling with non-trivial recovery widens from 2--3 ranks
at $K{=}8$ to 12--13 ranks at $K{=}32$, and the ceiling itself falls with
$K$, from $1.000$ to $0.795$.

What this transfer exposes, and what the synthetic construction cannot
see, is a phenomenon distinct from capacity: on real text,
\emph{per-binding exactness} --- not the rank recruited --- is what
degrades with $K$, and it compounds geometrically with composition depth
($\sim\varepsilon^h$). We trace this decay to a single causal source.
Holding grammar, model, and loss fixed and varying only the embedding
source feeding the write-time key, every tested input geometry ---
learned, frozen-orthonormal, frozen real-embedding, Gram-matched ---
converges to the \emph{same} non-orthonormal write-time key attractor,
ruling out value geometry, NCE-crowding, and kernel precision as
competing accounts. A surgical existence proof completes the attribution:
bypassing the learned key projection entirely with a hand-built,
per-identity orthonormal key pin achieves $\mathrm{rec@0.9}=
1.00/1.00/1.00$ at $h{=}1/2/3$ ($K{=}32$), against a learned baseline of
$0.78/0.26/0.05$ --- proof that the architecture can represent and use the
exact solution, and that ordinary training does not find it.

Given that the exact solution exists and that soft interventions do not
reach it (Section~\ref{sec:soft-fixes}), we design, gate, and demonstrate a
differentiable fix: per-episode Newton-Schulz key orthogonalization at the
write site, launched only after a pre-registered drift-based simulator
predicted it would clear its bar. The fix lifts held-out four-hop recovery
at $K{=}16$ from a $0.42$--$0.47$ baseline to $0.95$--$1.00$ (3/3 seeds
admissible against a pre-registered, arm-specific criterion, clearing the
$\geq0.8$ minimum-publishable bar), and improves $K{=}32$ four-hop
recovery $\sim$45$\times$ ($0.009\to0.39$--$0.50$, 3/3 seeds fully
admissible on the \texttt{n\_iter=20} escalation, zero fallback steps),
narrowly missing the pre-registered $\geq0.5$ headline bar (mean $0.44$,
one seed at the line). The miss is not left unexplained: trained
cross-episode key drift at $K{=}32$ measures $0.9037$, inside the
pre-registered high-drift band ($<0.95$) an earlier adversarial round had
named and measured before this wave ran --- isolating cross-episode key
\emph{stability}, not within-episode orthogonality, as the remaining
bottleneck.

That residual motivates a natural follow-on: can SGD supply cross-episode
stability itself, via a learned, per-entity key anchor? We test this to
completion across four waves. The behavioral gain is real and
independently replicated --- $K{=}32$ four-hop recovery rises from a
freshly measured $0.4105$ baseline to $0.61$--$0.71$ across 9 seed-runs
spanning 3 distinct seed sets and 2 architecture variants --- but the
hypothesized mechanism is rejected (Outcome C): a purpose-built
per-entity engagement instrument shows fewer than 14--41\% of entities
individually engage at every measured leg, confirmed across two
independent mechanism-tier waves and immune to a specific rescue attempt
that was itself independently attacked and rejected. We then find the
actual explanation by pre-registered ablation, not speculation: a frozen,
never-trained anchor table --- random or frame-potential initialized ---
at the same blend weight matches or exceeds the learned table's own gain
($0.67$--$0.76$ vs.\ $0.61$--$0.71$). This is confirmed by ablation, not
merely suggested by it: the prediction that a frozen table should match
the gain if constancy alone is the channel was registered before the
confirming data existed, and it held. Constancy in the blend arithmetic,
not entity-specific learned content, is the active ingredient; SGD's only
demonstrated contribution is tuning a single blend-weight scalar. Finally,
we bound this fix's operating regime rather than leave it open-ended.
Extending the identical design to $K{=}48$ ($K/d{=}0.75$) shows both of
its own explanatory channels measurably active yet the fix still misses
its bar 0/3 seeds: the capacity curve is a cliff
($\sim1.00\to\sim0.65\to\sim0.02$), not a smooth decline, while the
theoretical ceiling itself declines only gently over the same range
($0.97\to0.94\to0.90$). We locate this transition's midpoint at
$x_0{=}0.5455$ (95\% CI $[0.5385,0.5513]$) at $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}64$;
show it is not a universal property of $K/d$ but dissolves entirely at
$d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}128$ across the identical ratio window ($h4{=}1.0$,
12/12 cells); directly test and exonerate anchor-table coherence as the
driving confound, at both a concentrated and a diffuse injection
structure, up to and beyond $d{=}64$'s own realized coherence band (every
cell flat at $h4{=}1.0$); and, with coherence excluded, test the one
remaining candidate --- raw state capacity --- directly, finding load
tolerance grows \emph{super-linearly} in $d_{\mathrm{state}}$ rather than
at a fixed ratio ($x_0(80){=}0.6779$, excluding the pre-registered
ratio-invariant band; $x_0(96)$ itself not yet resolved, from genuine
seed-level scatter rather than a data gap).

Every claim above was pre-registered, independently attacked, and --- for
the fix and its follow-on --- gated on a predicted outcome from a
committed instrument before any GPU spend. We do not narrate this as a
virtue in the abstract; we cite it where it did real work. A mini-audit
caught a target-index gather bug in the real-data grammar that had made
the binding task unsatisfiable as originally specced
(Section~\ref{sec:phenomenon}); the fix's own gating diagnostic caught a
cross-episode key-drift failure mode the original design missed
(Section~\ref{sec:the-fix}).

We are explicit about what this paper does not claim. We make no claim
about production Gated-DeltaNet at scale: every causal chapter above uses
a single head, single layer, sub-1M-to-low-single-digit-million-parameter
configuration. We make no claim that natural-language reasoning
\emph{requires} rank $K$; our corpus-generality evidence
(Section~\ref{sec:discussion}) is descriptive and interventional, not
premise-conditional causal. And we make no claim that the $h{=}21$
literal-depth decay mechanism is fixed by this paper's intervention --- it
is not, and we say so directly in Section~\ref{sec:results}.
