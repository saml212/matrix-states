\section{Soft Fixes Fail: The Attractor Is Robust}
\label{sec:soft-fixes}

% Full grounding: DELTANET_RD_EXACTNESS_DESIGN.md \S15 (18/18 cells);
% EXPERIMENT_LOG.md "Exactness Wave F (soft arms)" 2026-07-04.

Having attributed the write-time non-orthonormality to a training-time
attractor rather than to input geometry, we ask whether a soft,
differentiable push away from that attractor is enough to escape it. Two
pre-registered interventions get one registered shot each, with no
further tuning after launch (the anti-Goodhart cap honored throughout, so
this reads as a genuine test rather than ``we could have tried harder''):
F-geo-1, an orthogonality penalty $L_{\mathrm{orth}}$ at
$\lambda\in\{0.1,1.0\}$, and F-geo-2, population-level ZCA whitening.

\subsection{Result: One Attractor, Three Approach Angles}

All three interventions land in the same narrow basin
(Figure~\ref{fig:soft-fixes-epsilon-h}): key-Gram deviation of
$2.51$--$2.57$ at $K{=}32$ against the baseline's $2.71$--$2.77$, a
$3$--$8\%$ cleanup regardless of mechanism --- weak soft push, strong soft
push, or population-level decorrelation --- or of $\lambda$ (a
$10\times$ change buys nothing; the penalty saturates). Every
pre-registered bar is missed: the headline bar ($K{=}32,h{=}4\geq0.5$)
lands at a measured $0.014$--$0.026$, $\sim$20--25$\times$ below it; the
minimum-publishable bar ($K{=}16,h{=}4\geq0.8$) lands at
$0.465$--$0.516$, still $\sim$1.6--1.7$\times$ above what was achieved.
The $h{=}1$ no-sacrifice guard is satisfied everywhere --- these
interventions do what they promise, just not enough of it.

\subsection{The Proportionality Reading: Real, but Geometrically
Shrinking}

The Gram cleanup at $K{=}32$ is real, not noise --- every one of 9 seeds
lands below the baseline band --- but the recovery gain it buys shrinks
at every hop: $+0.07$ to $+0.10$ at $h{=}2$, $+0.04$ to $+0.05$ at
$h{=}3$, only $+0.005$ to $+0.017$ at $h{=}4$. This is exactly the shape
the $\varepsilon^h$ compounding law of Section~\ref{sec:phenomenon}
predicts: a modest, real reduction in per-binding $\varepsilon$ produces
a benefit that shrinks in reverse as composition depth $h$ grows.

\subsection{Architectural Robustness: Not a qk-Norm Artifact, and a
Gating Trend That Does Not Confirm}

Two further approach angles test a different axis: not train-time soft
pushes on the synthetic task, but an architectural-robustness
$2\times2$ (qk-l2-norm $\times$ Gated-DeltaNet-style decay) run at the
14M-parameter real-data LM rung of the scale ladder
(Section~\ref{sec:discussion}), read with the same-corpus key-Gram-deviation
probe against corrected same-corpus cross-seed noise floors
(openr1-mix-ext $2.244$, wikitext-mix-ext $2.217$): an $n{=}1$ screening
pass, followed by a pre-registered $n{=}3$ escalation on whichever axis's
screening delta exceeded the $2\sigma$ trigger. The attractor persists in
all four cells (same-corpus Gram deviation $18.2$--$25.2$, every cell
$\geq8\sigma$ above zero deviation in floor units) --- neither axis
removes it. qk-norm is exonerated at $n{=}3$-solid strength: every run in
this program already used the community-standard qk-l2-norm mitigation,
and turning it off is a within-noise null ($\Delta{=}{-}0.103{=}0.05\sigma$
against the corrected floor) --- the attractor is cross-key population
geometry, not the single-vector eigenvalue-stability axis qk-norm
conditions, which directly answers whether this is the known qk-norm
issue in disguise (it is not). Gated-DeltaNet-style decay shows a
direction-consistent amplification trend that did \emph{not} meet its own
pre-registered confirmation bar: the $n{=}1$ screening triggered escalation
at $+6.16{=}2.75\sigma$, but the $n{=}3$ escalation shrank to
$+4.31{=}1.92\sigma$, below the pre-registered $2\sigma$ bar of $4.489$
(all three paired seeds positive, exploratory Welch $p{=}0.062$) ---
reported as a trend, never a claim. Directionally, gated arms carry
\emph{more} deviation while training to \emph{lower} loss in every seed
($3.515$ vs.\ $3.682$ final train loss): a healthier-looking checkpoint
carries the pathology no better, if anything trend-level worse,
consistent with this section's one-attractor reading. Together these two
angles partially discharge the single-architecture-family limitation
named in Section~\ref{sec:discussion}: qk-norm is exonerated at $n{=}3$
and the Gated-DeltaNet-style decay direction now has a trend-level
answer at 14M, though DeltaProduct and production-scale gated
configurations remain untested.

\subsection{Honest Conclusion}

This is a pre-registered negative branch, not a failed pilot: a dominant
mechanism whose matching fix does not move the frontier is itself a
publishable finding. The demonstration-claim tier verdict is false for
all three soft arms, stated plainly; the weaker true claim earned is a
genuine, causal (premise-conditional) but small write-geometry cleanup,
with recovery moving proportionally in the predicted direction.
F-geo-1's own key-Gram number is compromised as premise-evidence, since
it is directly optimized by $L_{\mathrm{orth}}$, so its verdict rests on
behavioral $\mathrm{rec@0.9}$ bars alone; F-geo-2 (ZCA) carries no such
optimization carve-out and lands in the same basin anyway --- itself
evidence the carve-out is not hiding a different outcome for the other
two arms.
