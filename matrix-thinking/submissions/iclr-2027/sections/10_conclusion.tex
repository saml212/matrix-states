\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Restate the thesis in the paper's own final voice: capacity and
    exactness are separable, measurable, and separately fixable
    properties of a fast-weight architecture's learned state --- this
    paper is the first to show the gap exists on real text, name its
    cause (a two-part necessary-and-sufficient mechanism: within-episode
    orthogonality and cross-episode key stability), demonstrate a
    mechanism-matched fix for the first part, and fully characterize a
    completed attempt at the second part: a real fix whose actual
    mechanism differs from its hypothesized one, confirmed by ablation,
    with its working regime measured and bounded.
  \item Close the causal chain explicitly: bar hit at $K{=}16$ (3/3);
    $\sim$45$\times$ at $K{=}32$ at full admissibility with the narrow
    bar miss attributed to outcome F; independently corroborated by the
    2$\times$2. \textbf{The named follow-on (learned cross-episode key
    anchoring) ran to completion across four waves, not future work:} a
    real, independently-replicated $K{=}32$ $h{=}4$ gain
    ($0.41 \to 0.61$--$0.71$) whose hypothesized entity-alignment
    mechanism is rejected (Outcome C, confirmed twice, immune to a
    rejected rescue attempt), whose actual mechanism is confirmed by
    ablation (a frozen random table matches or beats the learned one ---
    constancy, not alignment, is the active ingredient), and whose
    operating regime is now measured (works at $K/d{\leq}0.5$, fails at
    $K/d{=}0.75$, both explanatory channels active-but-insufficient at
    the miss, a cliff not a smooth decline). \textbf{That cliff is now a
    complete three-act finding, not left as a single observed boundary:}
    LOCATED to $x_0{=}0.5455$ (95\% CI $[0.5385,0.5513]$) at
    $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}64$; shown to DISSOLVE, not merely shift, at
    $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}128$ across the identical $K/d$ window
    ($h4{=}1.0$, 12/12 cells); and its leading single-variable confound
    (anchor-table coherence) directly tested and EXONERATED by injecting
    controlled, frozen coherence past $d{=}64$'s own realized band under a
    concentrated structure ($h4{=}1.0$, 10/10 cells, no exception). No
    loose ends on the mechanism --- the behavioral result, the rejected
    hypothesis, the confirmed explanation, the capacity boundary, its
    precise location, its dissolution with scale, and the honest
    non-resolution of what \emph{does} drive it (overlap structure vs.\
    raw state capacity, both still live) are reported as the seven
    separate things they are. \textbf{The raw-state-capacity candidate has
    since been tested directly at two further $d_{\mathrm{state}}$'s and
    the result is super-linear, not ratio-invariant:} re-fitting the same
    transition at $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}80$ locates it at
    $x_0{=}0.6756$ (95\% CI $[0.6620,0.6868]$), cleanly outside the
    pre-registered ratio-invariant band $[0.4745,0.6165]$; at
    $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}96$ the transition does not localize at all
    inside the tested window (load tolerance $>0.72$, $K/d{=}0.71875$).
    This rules out ``the cliff sits at a fixed $K/d$ fraction'' as cleanly
    as the $d{=}128$ dissolution ruled out ``the cliff never moves,'' but
    the exact functional form of the growth (and the precise
    $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}96$ transition point) remains open --- a
    wide-grid follow-up is designed and power-checked, not yet built or
    launched, and its result is not claimed here.
  \item \textbf{Closing note on scale.} The phenomenon this paper studies
    is not confined to the scale it is studied at: a controlled
    14M$\to$98M$\to$392M$\to$1.31B ladder, on a single held-fixed data
    mix, shows the write-geometry deviation climbing monotonically across
    all four points (span-fraction $0.248 \to 0.344 \to 0.389 \to
    0.455$), a \textbf{pure-scale, now-complete} finding --- a positive
    result in its own right: the failure mode this paper studies is real,
    persists rather than being washed out by scale, and is now precisely
    characterized through 1.31B parameters. \textbf{Disclosure:} the
    1.31B rung self-terminated at a designed internal timeout at
    $\approx$84.7\% of its token-matched budget (steps
    $155{,}081/155{,}028$ of $183{,}105$, a clean shutdown, not a crash,
    with no optimizer-state resume available); a plateau check at the
    final four logged checkpoints shows the span-fraction reading flat to
    mildly declining ($-0.003$ over the last 25k steps, against the
    $+0.066$ rung-2$\to$rung-3 increment it would be revising), so the
    harvested 155k-step reading is not sensitive to the shortfall. A
    substantially larger version of the same signature is present in
    deployed production fixed-state models,
    not yet attributable to the same mechanism. A companion attempt to
    install the demonstrated fix mechanism itself inside a
    free-running-text setting (Track B) is, independently, blocked twice
    on numerical-stability grounds --- named honestly as a current
    boundary, not smoothed over.
  \item Close on the honest scope: one architecture family, small scale
    for the core causal chapters (partially addressed by the pure-scale
    ladder above), the $K{=}32$ headline bar honestly missed (narrowly,
    with a named cause), one mechanism ($h{=}21$ depth-compounding) left
    explicitly unfixed, and the anchoring follow-on's own fix bounded to
    $K/d{\leq}0.5$ rather than open-ended.
  \item DO NOT end on a generalization claim beyond what \S\ref{sec:discussion}
    licenses --- the closing paragraph is the highest-risk overclaiming
    surface in the whole paper. In particular, do not let the anchoring
    arc's resolution read as bigger than it is: it closes one named
    residual (cross-episode key stability) with a simpler-than-expected
    mechanism, it does not extend the fix past $K/d{=}0.5$, and it does
    not touch $h{=}21$ or full-scale transfer.
\end{itemize}
