Rank recruitment and rank \emph{exactness} are different achievements: a
fixed-state linear-attention model (DeltaNet) trained end-to-end on real
tokenized language reliably recruits the provably-necessary rank at every
tested capacity $K$, but the per-binding exactness of what it writes into
that rank decays sharply with $K$ and compounds geometrically ($\sim
\varepsilon^h$) under composition. We trace the decay to a single,
measurable, structurally-robust cause --- a non-orthonormal write-time key
attractor every input geometry converges to --- show two soft interventions
cannot escape it, and demonstrate a differentiable structural fix that
restores near-perfect compositional recall at the pre-registered
minimum-publishable bar ($K{=}16$) and transforms (by $\sim$50$\times$) the
