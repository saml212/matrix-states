\section{The Fix: Structural, Differentiable Orthogonalization (F-geo-3)}
\label{sec:the-fix}

% Full grounding: DELTANET_RD_EXACTNESS_DESIGN.md \S14 (esp. \S14.0, \S14.1,
% \S14.6, \S14.10).

Two facts motivate what follows, not engineering for its own sake: the
exact solution exists and composes perfectly (i-strong,
Section~\ref{sec:mechanism}), and SGD does not find it under any soft
push tried (Section~\ref{sec:soft-fixes}). If a soft penalty cannot move
the write-time key population out of its attractor, the natural next
step is to make orthogonality structural rather than encouraged.

\subsection{Design}

We insert a cubic Newton-Schulz / Bj\"orck-Bowie iteration at the
$k_{\mathrm{eff}}$ site, $X_{t+1} = 1.5X_t - 0.5(X_tX_t^\top)X_t$, which
converges quadratically to the nearest orthonormal-row matrix given
operator norm $\leq1$ at $t{=}0$ --- guaranteed here by $\sqrt{K}$
pre-scaling against the existing unit-row-norm invariant. We rejected two
natural alternatives on explicit grounds: Gram-Schmidt is order-dependent
and would reintroduce, one level up, exactly the write-history asymmetry
this mechanism is meant to remove; QR is order-dependent for the same
reason and additionally backward-unstable near collinear input --- the
exact regime an undertrained embedding table produces. Newton-Schulz is
order-equivariant (permuting the input rows permutes the iterates
identically, so no row is privileged) and differentiable through ordinary
matrix multiplications, requiring no linear-algebra-specific backward
formula.

\subsection{The Gating Discipline}

A two-round independent attack found a load-bearing failure mode the
original design missed: cross-episode key-\emph{identity} drift.
Orthogonalizing correctly \emph{within} an episode does not guarantee the
\emph{same} key is produced across separate episodes of the same entity.
This became a pre-registered Wave~$-1$ gating diagnostic with its own
committed, CPU-smoke-tested simulator (\texttt{geo3\_simulator.py}) and a
launch-read rule: Wave~1 GPU spend was authorized only after the
simulator, fed measured cross-episode key drift of $0.94$ at $K{=}16$
and $0.90$ at $K{=}32$, predicted $K16\ h4{=}1.00$ and $K32\
h4{=}0.77$. This is a second concrete instance of the pre-registration
discipline named in Section~\ref{sec:setup}: the process is worth
narrating only insofar as it is paired with the specific finding it
caught, and here it caught a failure mode the design itself had missed.

\subsection{The Substitute Admission Stack}

F-geo-3 makes the standard key-Gram/alignment premise-validity
instruments tautological by construction --- orthogonality is true and
alignment is $1.0$ by construction, not by measurement. A substitute
criterion, pre-registered before any F-geo-3 data existed, applies
instead: value-side salvage, Newton-Schulz convergence without
\texttt{eigh} fallback, finite loss, and a task-performance floor
($\geq$50\% loss improvement and $h{=}1$ in-distribution
$\mathrm{rec@0.9}\geq0.5$). With the $K{=}32$ \texttt{n\_iter=20}
escalation cells (Section~\ref{sec:results}) passing every substitute
criterion including zero fallback steps, and $K{=}16$ passing 3/3 from
the start, this caveat drops from headline asterisk to footnote --- but
the footnote still names both admission stacks and both realized pass
rates wherever geo3 and non-geo3 seed counts appear side by side, per
the pre-registration's own requirement. The fallback-irrelevance finding
of Section~\ref{sec:results} shows the one criterion that ever
disqualified a seed was conservative, not explanatory.

\subsection{The Anchoring Follow-On}

Section~\ref{sec:results} shows that F-geo-3's own $K{=}32$ result is a
$\sim$45$\times$ improvement that narrowly misses its pre-registered bar,
and attributes the miss to a second, distinct mechanism: cross-episode
key drift measured at $0.9037$, inside the pre-registered high-drift
band. That residual motivates a natural follow-on question, which we
test to completion rather than leave open: can SGD supply cross-episode
key \emph{stability} itself, via a learned, per-entity key anchor blended
into the key before geo3's Newton-Schulz pass? We ran this program across
four waves plus one rejected rescore attempt, $\approx$55.83 of its own
80 GPU-h budget, with no further waves scheduled. The headline, in one
sentence: the resulting behavioral gain is real and independently
replicated, the entity-alignment mechanism hypothesized to explain it is
rejected by a purpose-built engagement instrument, the actual explanation
is confirmed by a pre-registered ablation (a frozen, never-trained anchor
table at the same blend weight matches or exceeds the learned table's own
gain --- constancy in the blend arithmetic, not entity-specific learned
content, is the active ingredient), and the fix's operating regime is
then measured directly rather than left open-ended, holding at
$K/d\leq0.5$ and failing at $K/d{=}0.75$ where the capacity curve
completes as a cliff rather than a smooth decline. The one open follow-on
this program itself named --- value-crowding at $K/d{=}0.75$ --- is a new,
unregistered direction requiring its own design/attack/build/audit cycle,
not a continuation of this one. The full numbers, gating history, and
figures for this arc are reported together in
Section~\ref{sec:results} and Section~\ref{sec:phenomenon}'s $K{=}48$
rider, rather than split across two places: a rejected hypothesis and a
confirmed-by-ablation alternative are both reported, not just the
flattering one, and the alternative's own operating regime is now
bounded, closing what was, at an earlier stage of this project, this
paper's single most open thread.
