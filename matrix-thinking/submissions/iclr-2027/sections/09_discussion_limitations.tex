\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

% Full content: NARRATIVE.md \S6. State every item undiluted --- this is

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Small scale.} $d_{\mathrm{model}}$ in the low hundreds,
    $d_{\mathrm{state}}{=}64$, sub-1M--low-single-digit-M parameters (the
    core causal chapters). \textbf{Partial evidence, not a resolution:} a
    controlled 14M$\to$98M$\to$392M scaling ladder (Tier 2,
    geometry-only, a clean 3-point read on a single held-fixed corpus mix,
    Fig.~9 Panel A) finds the write-geometry attractor does NOT dissolve
    with scale --- it WORSENS monotonically (span-fraction
    $0.248 \to 0.344 \to 0.389$), a \textbf{pure-scale} claim. A fourth,
    1.31B-parameter rung was running as this draft was prepared
    \todo{[PENDING RUNG-3] insert the 1.31B span-fraction reading and its
    position relative to the 3-point trend the moment Track C rung-3
    reports; update Fig.~9 Panel A's fourth point and this sentence
    together --- see NARRATIVE.md \S9 item 3.}; this item's core claim
    (untested at production scale for compositional recovery, not just
    geometry) stands regardless of that reading.
  \item \textbf{Single architecture family.} Vanilla, single-head,
    single-layer DeltaNet only. Gated DeltaNet (the actual Qwen3-Next
    production config), DeltaProduct's multi-Householder generalization,
    untested. $H{=}1$/single-layer was a deliberate gate-simplicity
    decision, not evidence of $H{=}1$-specificity (ReserveMH's
    generality result was run on the SYNTHETIC task only).
  \item \textbf{$h{=}21$ literal-depth decay remains unexplained and
    unfixed.} F-geo-3 demonstrably does not touch it --- a named,
    scoped-out mechanism (iteration-compounding), not folded into ``the
    fix works.''
  \item \textbf{The $K{=}32$ headline bar is not met, and its named
    follow-on has now run to completion with a fully resolved, if more
    complex than hoped, result.} The fix is fully admissible at BOTH $K$
    ($K{=}16$ 3/3; $K{=}32$ 3/3 on the \texttt{n\_iter=20} escalation,
    zero fallback steps), but the pre-registered $\geq 0.5$ headline bar
    at $K{=}32$ is narrowly missed: $h{=}4 = 0.504/0.416/0.390$, mean
    $0.44$, 1/3 seeds at the line. The miss is attributed to outcome F
    (trained cross-episode key drift $0.9037$ in the pre-registered HIGH
    band). \textbf{The learned anchor-table follow-on designed to close
    that residual (\S\ref{sec:the-fix}) has now run to completion across
    four waves,} not future work: it lifts $K{=}32$ $h{=}4$ recovery from
    a freshly-measured $0.4105$ baseline to $0.61$--$0.71$ (independently
    replicated, 9 seed-runs across 3 seed sets, 2 architecture variants);
    its own per-entity engagement instrument reads under 14--41\%
    engagement at every leg, confirming Outcome C (``mechanism not
    engaged'') at increasing rigor across two waves, immune to a rejected
    rescue attempt. \textbf{The actual explanation was then found and
    confirmed by ablation:} a frozen, never-trained anchor table (random
    or frame-potential init) at the same blend weight matches or exceeds
    the learned table's own gain --- constancy in the blend arithmetic,
    not entity-specific learned content, is the active ingredient; SGD's
    only demonstrated role is tuning the blend weight. \textbf{The fix's
    operating regime is now measured, not open-ended:} a capacity-curve
    extension to $K{=}48$ ($K/d{=}0.75$) shows the identical fix fully
    admissible, both of its own named explanatory channels measurably
    active, and the bar still missed 0/3 seeds --- the curve is a cliff
    ($\sim$1.00 $\to$ $\sim$0.65 $\to$ $\sim$0.02), not a smooth decline.
    State plainly: the behavioral $K{=}32$ $h{=}4$ gain is real and its
    cause is now known (constancy, confirmed by ablation); the
    entity-alignment hypothesis originally proposed for it is rejected;
    and the fix's regime is bounded at $K/d{\leq}0.5$. This item, open at
    an earlier stage of this project, is now closed.
  \item \textbf{The $K\approx d/2$ structural boundary is now a measured
    cliff, not merely observed.} The completed capacity-curve extension
    (item 4 above) turns this into a fully measured three-point curve
    with both of the design's own candidate explanatory channels checked
    directly and found active-but-insufficient at $K{=}48$ --- the open
    question is no longer ``does it break past $d/2$'' (yes, measured)
    but ``why does the break happen so sharply, between $K/d{=}0.5$ and
    $K/d{=}0.75$, when the theoretical ceiling itself declines only
    gently over the same range.'' The design's own named, not-yet-tested
    candidate for this is value-crowding (more entities packed into the
    same fixed value dimensionality reducing per-entity value-recovery
    headroom independent of key-side conditioning), which would require a
    fresh design to test.
  \item \textbf{Scale-transfer to a full pretrained LLM checkpoint is now
    PARTIALLY measured, and the fix mechanism's transfer to free-running
    text is separately tested and blocked.} Wave 2 establishes the
    truncation-damage phenomenon generalizes to naturalistic LM
    pretraining at small scale (descriptive+interventional tier only).
    Beyond that: (a) on a controlled 14M$\to$98M$\to$392M parameter
    ladder, on a single held-fixed data mix, the write-geometry attractor
    does not dissolve --- it WORSENS monotonically, a \textbf{pure-scale}
    finding (Fig.~9 Panel A), not yet extended to compositional recovery
    at scale, with a 1.31B-parameter fourth rung
    \todo{[PENDING RUNG-3] state whether it continued climbing,
    plateaued, or reversed once it reports.}; (b) a much larger version
    of the SAME non-orthonormal write-geometry signature is measurable
    directly in production-scale pretrained fixed-state models (RWKV-7
    1.5B, Falcon-Mamba-7B; Fig.~9 Panel B); (c) but a matched
    no-fixed-state negative control (Qwen2.5-1.5B) shows the SAME
    magnitude, so (b) is explicitly NOT attributable to the delta-rule
    write mechanism specifically at this measurement tier; and (d) a
    companion attempt to install the SAME fix mechanism inside a
    free-running-text LM (Track B) is blocked before any interaction
    claim can be tested --- a duplicate-key numerical-stability smoke
    overshoots its bar by 63$\times$ with a probative positive control,
    compounding the original $\beta$-uniformity gate refusal; the
    selectivity-only readout that DID complete is INCONCLUSIVE (a split
    verdict across corpora). Net honest statement: the phenomenon
    persists where it can be controlled for and now scales purely with
    parameter count (a), a phenomenologically similar signature exists at
    production scale (b) but causal attribution there remains open and
    confounded (c), and the fix itself has not yet been shown to transfer
    to a free-running-text setting at all (d).
  \item \textbf{The admission-stack asymmetry, now footnote-level.}
    Because F-geo-3 makes the standard instruments tautological, a
    substitute pre-registered criterion applies to its seeds. With every
    substitute criterion passed at both $K$ (including zero fallback
    steps on the escalation cells), this drops from headline asterisk to
    footnote --- but still name both stacks and both realized pass rates
    wherever geo3 and non-geo3 seed counts appear side by side, because
    the two stacks filter different failure modes and their relative
    selectivity was never empirically equated.
  \item \textbf{The 2$\times$2's ``stable'' cells lean on existence-tier
    or bundled arms --- no longer an open dependency, a closed, mixed
    answer.} The 2$\times$2 (Fig.~5) that demonstrates orthogonality and
    stability are jointly necessary uses i-strong (pool-restricted to
    $32{\leq}d_{\mathrm{state}}$) for the ``both together'' cell and
    frozen-embedding arms for the ``stable, not orthogonal'' cells. The
    key-anchoring program (\S\ref{sec:the-fix}) was built specifically to
    test whether a full-vocabulary, gradient-discovered anchor mechanism
    can reach the ``jointly sufficient'' regime, and it has run to
    completion across four waves with a final, closed answer: the wave
    produces a real $K{=}32$ $h{=}4$ behavioral gain
    ($0.4105 \to 0.61$--$0.71$) but NOT via the hypothesized
    entity-alignment channel (Outcome C, confirmed twice); the gain's
    actual cause is confirmed by ablation to be constancy in the blend
    arithmetic, not learned content; and this alternate mechanism's own
    regime is bounded (works at $K/d{\leq}0.5$, fails at $K/d{=}0.75$).
    So: the 2$\times$2's ``jointly sufficient'' corner still rests on the
    hand-built i-strong pin for a \emph{causal} demonstration at $K{=}32$;
    the \emph{practical}, SGD-adjacent route tested to reach that corner
    does not use the hypothesized mechanism, works via a simpler
    (constancy-only) route instead, and that alternate route's own
    working regime is now known. A closed, three-part answer, stated as
    such.
  \item \textbf{The key-anchoring program is now fully complete, and its
    complete arc is part of this paper's evidence base.} Every claim
    above about ``the residual mechanism'' (outcome F, the 2$\times$2)
    still rests on the outcome-F attribution and the 2$\times$2, not on
    the anchoring program's numbers, for the causal MECHANISM claim about
    why F-geo-3 misses its $K{=}32$ bar. What the anchoring program
    contributes, now completely: a real, independently-replicated
    $K{=}32$ $h{=}4$ behavioral improvement; a rejected entity-alignment
    hypothesis (Outcome C, confirmed twice); a confirmed alternate
    explanation (constancy suffices, by ablation); a measured capacity
    boundary ($K/d{\leq}0.5$ works, $K/d{=}0.75$ does not); and a
    demonstrated instance of this project's own gating discipline both
    catching an attempted post-hoc rescue of a null result AND
    pre-registering and then confirming a falsifiable account of a
    surprising positive result. Report all of this, not just the
    flattering parts.
\end{enumerate}
